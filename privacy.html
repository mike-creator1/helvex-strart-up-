Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy describes how HelveX AG ("HelveX", "we", "us") collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal data when you visit our website, use our services, or interact with our platform. It applies globally and is harmonised with the GDPR (EU), the Swiss FADP / nFADP, and the CCPA / CPRA (California).

EffectiveApril 2026 Last updatedApril 2026 Version1.0 Governing lawSwitzerland
§ 01

Scope and applicability

This Privacy Policy applies to personal data that HelveX AG ("HelveX") processes about you in connection with the helvex.ai website, the HelveX platform (including the workspace, the Ether, Nexus, and Prometheus models, and the managed engagement practice), and any communications you have with us. It applies whether you are a customer (or an employee or agent of a customer), a visitor to our website, a recipient of our marketing communications, a candidate applying for a role at HelveX, or a member of the public who interacts with us in some other capacity.

This Policy distinguishes two roles HelveX may play with respect to personal data. When you visit the helvex.ai website, sign up for an account, communicate with us, or otherwise interact with us directly, HelveX acts as a controller of the personal data we collect. When a HelveX customer (the "Customer") uses the HelveX platform to process personal data of its own users, employees, or other data subjects, the Customer is the controller of that data and HelveX is a processor acting on the Customer's documented instructions. The terms governing HelveX's processor role are set out in our Data Processing Addendum.

This Policy does not cover the practices of third parties whose websites or services may be linked from helvex.ai or accessible from within the platform. Where the platform integrates with third-party services at a customer's direction (for example, an integration with the customer's own knowledge sources or downstream systems), the third party's own privacy practices apply to the data exchanged with that third party.

§ 03

Information we collect

HelveX collects personal data in three principal ways: information you provide to us directly, information generated by your use of our services, and information we receive from third parties. We describe each category below.

3.1 Information you provide

You may provide personal data when you create an account, configure a workspace, communicate with us, or use specific features. The categories of personal data you may provide include:

  • Account information: your full name, your work email address, a password (stored only in cryptographically hashed form using a modern key-derivation function), your organisation name, your job title, and your country.
  • Billing information: your organisation's billing address, your billing contact, your VAT or other tax identifier, and payment-method details. Raw payment-card numbers are collected and processed by our PCI-DSS-compliant payment processor; HelveX retains only a tokenised reference and the last four digits of the card.
  • Support and communications: the content of any messages you send to us through the support channel, the contact form on helvex.ai, or otherwise; the metadata associated with that communication (sender, recipient, time, channel).
  • Content you upload: any documents, prompts, files, or other materials you upload to the workspace or otherwise submit to the platform.

3.2 Information generated by your use

Some personal data is generated automatically as a consequence of your interaction with the platform. This category includes:

  • Prompts and completions: the prompts you submit to a model and the corresponding completions returned. These constitute Customer Content where the Customer is a controller; see Section 7 below for how we handle them.
  • Telemetry: pseudonymised event data describing how you use the workspace (which surfaces you visit, which features you trigger, performance metrics). Telemetry payloads exclude prompts and completions.
  • Error reports: technical error information generated when the application encounters an exception, scrubbed of Customer Content before transmission.
  • Audit logs: records of consequential actions you take within the workspace, including authentication, configuration changes, and data access; see Section 8 of the Trust Center for detail.
  • Device and connection information: IP address, user-agent string, device identifiers, language and locale, time zone, and other technical signals associated with each request.
Commitment

HelveX does not use Customer Content, including prompts and completions, to train our base models. Enterprise customers may additionally configure a zero-retention posture in which prompts and completions are processed only in volatile memory and are not persisted at rest. See Section 7.

3.3 Information from third parties

We receive limited personal data from third parties as follows: when you authenticate through a single-sign-on identity provider that your organisation has connected to its tenant, we receive the attributes your administrator has configured the identity provider to release (typically your name, email address, and group memberships); when our payment processor confirms a payment on your organisation's behalf, we receive a confirmation token, the last four digits of the card, and the billing country; when we receive marketing-qualified-lead enrichment from publicly available business databases, we may match a corporate email address you have submitted to a business profile to support contextual outreach.

§ 04

Cookies and similar technologies

HelveX uses cookies and similar technologies on the helvex.ai website and within the workspace. We use cookies in three categories only, and we do not use cookies for advertising purposes.

Strictly necessary cookies

These cookies are required for the website and the workspace to function. They include session cookies (which keep you logged in), cross-site-request-forgery prevention tokens, and load-balancer routing cookies. Strictly necessary cookies are not subject to consent under GDPR or the ePrivacy Directive because their use is genuinely necessary for the service you have requested.

Preferences cookies

These cookies remember your settings and preferences, such as language selection, theme (light or dark), and dismissal state for notification banners. Preferences cookies improve usability and are set on first interaction unless you have configured your browser to reject non-essential cookies.

Analytics cookies

Analytics cookies provide aggregated insight into how the website and the workspace are used. Where required by applicable law, we obtain consent for analytics cookies through a cookie banner; you may decline analytics cookies without affecting your ability to use our services. We use first-party analytics in preference to third-party analytics wherever feasible, to limit data sharing.

We do not use cookies, pixels, or other technologies for cross-site advertising, behavioural targeting, or sale of personal data to third-party advertising networks. We do not participate in advertising-identifier ecosystems.

§ 05

How we use information

HelveX uses personal data for the following purposes. For each purpose, we identify the categories of data involved and the corresponding legal basis (where the GDPR or Swiss FADP applies); legal bases are summarised in Section 6.

  • Service delivery: providing the platform, processing prompts, returning completions, persisting your account and workspace state, and enabling collaboration features. Categories: account information, content you upload, prompts and completions. Legal basis: performance of a contract.
  • Authentication and security: verifying your identity, detecting and preventing unauthorised access, abuse, fraud, and security incidents. Categories: device and connection information, authentication metadata, audit logs. Legal basis: legitimate interests; legal obligation under applicable security and breach-notification law.
  • Debugging and reliability: investigating errors, diagnosing reliability issues, performing root-cause analyses. Categories: error reports, telemetry, audit logs. Legal basis: legitimate interests.
  • Billing and accounting: invoicing, processing payments, tax reporting, dunning. Categories: billing information, account information. Legal basis: performance of a contract; legal obligation under Swiss commercial-code accounting rules.
  • Communication: responding to support requests, sending transactional emails (account verification, security alerts, service announcements), and, with appropriate legal basis, sending optional product updates and the monthly digest. Categories: account information, support and communications. Legal basis: performance of a contract for transactional communications; consent or legitimate interests for optional marketing communications.
  • Legal compliance: complying with applicable law, responding to legitimate requests from public authorities, and enforcing our Terms of Service and Acceptable Use Policy. Categories: any data necessary for the compliance obligation. Legal basis: legal obligation; legitimate interests.
  • Abuse detection: identifying and acting on misuse of the platform contrary to our Acceptable Use Policy, including automated classification of platform outputs for harmful-content categories. Categories: telemetry, aggregated platform signals; in serious cases, specific prompts or completions. Legal basis: legitimate interests; legal obligation.
  • Product improvement: developing new features, improving existing features, and conducting research on the platform's behaviour. We use aggregated, pseudonymised platform signals for this purpose. We do not use Customer Content (prompts, completions, files) to train our base models. Categories: telemetry. Legal basis: legitimate interests.
§ 06

Legal bases

Under Article 6(1) of the GDPR (and analogous provisions of the Swiss FADP / nFADP), HelveX must identify a lawful basis for each act of processing personal data. We rely on the following bases, individually or in combination, as identified for each purpose in Section 5.

Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b))

We rely on this basis to deliver the Services you have requested, to operate your account, to process your payments, and to perform the obligations we owe to you under the Terms of Service and any applicable order form.

Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f))

We rely on legitimate interests for processing activities that are not strictly necessary to perform a contract but are reasonably expected by data subjects and proportionate to the rights and freedoms at stake. The interests we pursue include the security of our platform, the prevention of fraud and abuse, the reliability of our services, and the improvement of features that benefit customers as a whole. We have conducted documented balancing tests for each processing activity relying on legitimate interests; these are available to data subjects on request to dpo@helvex.ai.

Legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c))

We rely on legal obligation to comply with mandatory record-keeping requirements (such as the ten-year retention of accounting records under Swiss Code of Obligations Article 958f), with breach-notification requirements under GDPR Articles 33 and 34 and equivalent provisions of Swiss FADP / nFADP, and with valid legal process from competent authorities.

Consent (Art. 6(1)(a))

Where applicable law requires consent (for example, for analytics cookies under the ePrivacy Directive, or for optional marketing communications in certain jurisdictions), we obtain consent through an explicit, informed, and freely given act. Consent can be withdrawn at any time with effect for the future by contacting dpo@helvex.ai or by using the unsubscribe controls in our communications.

§ 07

Customer Content and AI inputs/outputs

This section describes how HelveX handles "Customer Content", the term we use for content uploaded to the platform by a Customer or generated through the Customer's use of the Services, including prompts submitted to models and completions returned. Because Customer Content typically contains personal data of the Customer's own users or third parties, its handling is one of the most consequential privacy questions our customers and their data subjects ask.

Definition

"Customer Content" means: (a) any data, files, documents, prompts, instructions, or other materials submitted to the Services by or on behalf of the Customer; (b) any completions, outputs, summaries, or other artifacts generated by the Services in response to such submissions; and (c) any persisted state of agents, projects, knowledge bases, or spaces created within the Customer's tenant.

Processing for service delivery only

HelveX processes Customer Content solely to provide the Services to the Customer. Specifically, we process Customer Content to deliver prompts to the relevant model, to return completions, to render the workspace, to enforce access controls and quotas, to enforce the Acceptable Use Policy through automated abuse-detection systems, and to comply with applicable legal obligations.

No training on Customer Content

HelveX does not use Customer Content (including prompts, completions, files, agent state, or knowledge-base contents) to train, fine-tune, or otherwise improve the base capabilities of our models. This is a contractual commitment to Customers under the Data Processing Addendum and is operationalised through technical controls that prevent Customer Content from being routed into training pipelines.

Commitment

Customer Content is never used to train HelveX base models. This commitment applies to all Customers on all plans, by default, with no opt-out required.

Retention defaults

Default retention for Customer Content within the workspace is governed by the Customer's tenant configuration. Out of the box, chat threads and agent traces are retained for 30 days; the Customer's administrator may shorten this period or extend it (subject to plan limits). Files uploaded to Knowledge are retained until the Customer deletes them. Persisted state of Projects, Agents, and Spaces is retained until the Customer deletes the relevant resource.

Zero-retention option

Enterprise customers may configure a zero-retention posture in which prompts and completions are processed only in volatile memory during the inference request and are not persisted at rest. In zero-retention mode, the request is logged at the audit-trail level (actor, timestamp, model identifier, input and output sizes) but the request and response bodies are not retained beyond the lifetime of the request.

Customer instructions

Where Customer Content contains personal data of third parties for which the Customer is the controller, HelveX acts as a processor and processes that personal data only on the Customer's documented instructions, as set out in the Data Processing Addendum. The Customer is responsible for ensuring it has a valid legal basis for submitting personal data to HelveX and for making any disclosures to its own data subjects required by applicable law.

Legal compliance and law enforcement

Our standard practice is: to require the request in writing; to verify the requester's identity and authority; to challenge requests that are overbroad, illegal under Swiss or EU law, or unsupported by appropriate process; and to notify the affected Customer of the request unless prohibited by law. HelveX publishes a transparency report annually summarising the number and category of legal requests received and our response, to the extent permitted by law.

If HelveX is involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets, personal data may be transferred to a successor or acquirer as part of that transaction. The successor will be bound to honour the commitments in this Privacy Policy as to any personal data transferred, or to provide reasonable notice and choice if material changes are proposed.

With consent or at your direction

We share personal data with third parties where you direct us to do so (for example, when you enable an integration that requires data to flow to a downstream service) or where you have given explicit consent.

Aggregated and de-identified data

We may share aggregated or de-identified data that cannot reasonably be associated with an identified or identifiable individual. Such data is not personal data under GDPR or Swiss FADP.

We do not sell personal data

HelveX does not sell personal data within the meaning of CCPA/CPRA or any analogous concept under other data-protection law. We do not exchange personal data for monetary or other valuable consideration.

§ 09

International transfers

HelveX is headquartered in Switzerland and operates production infrastructure in multiple regions. Where personal data is transferred from one jurisdiction to another, the transfer is subject to the legal mechanisms described in this section.

Transfers within the EEA / EU

Personal data of EEA data subjects is processed primarily within the European Economic Area (EU-Central regions, including Frankfurt). Transfers between Member States of the EEA are permitted without additional safeguards under the GDPR.

Transfers from the EEA to third countries

Where personal data of EEA data subjects is transferred to a country outside the EEA, the transfer relies on one of the following mechanisms: (a) a European Commission adequacy decision under Article 45 GDPR; (b) the European Commission Standard Contractual Clauses (Decision (EU) 2021/914 of 4 June 2021), supplemented by appropriate technical and organisational measures including encryption in transit and at rest, pseudonymisation where feasible, and contractual access restrictions on the data importer; or (c) where neither of the above applies, a derogation under Article 49 GDPR (for example, explicit consent or necessity for the performance of a contract).

Transfers of Swiss personal data

Personal data of Swiss data subjects is subject to the Swiss FADP / nFADP. Where Swiss personal data is transferred to a country outside Switzerland, the transfer relies on (a) a Federal Council recognition of adequacy; (b) Standard Contractual Clauses supplemented by the Swiss Addendum recognised by the Swiss Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC); or (c) another lawful basis under Article 16 nFADP.

UK transfers

For transfers from the United Kingdom, HelveX relies on UK adequacy decisions, the UK International Data Transfer Agreement (IDTA), or the UK Addendum to the EU Standard Contractual Clauses, as appropriate.

Supplementary measures

Following Schrems II and subsequent guidance from the European Data Protection Board, HelveX has assessed the laws of importing countries and implemented supplementary measures where the assessment identified residual risk. These measures include strong encryption with keys managed within the export jurisdiction, contractual commitments to challenge unlawful disclosure requests, and operational segregation of personal data from access by personnel located in countries lacking adequate protection.

§ 10

Retention

HelveX retains personal data only as long as necessary for the purposes set out in this Privacy Policy or as required by applicable law. Specific retention periods are summarised below.

  • Account information: retained for the lifetime of the account plus 30 days after closure, after which the account record is deleted or anonymised. Some derived account information (for example, account identifiers in audit logs) is retained for the audit-log retention period.
  • Customer Content: retained according to the Customer's tenant configuration (default 30 days for chat threads and agent traces; until deletion for Knowledge files, Project state, Agent state, and Space state). For enterprise customers with zero-retention configuration, Customer Content is not persisted at rest at all.
  • Billing records: retained for a minimum of ten (10) years from the end of the financial year, as required by Article 958f of the Swiss Code of Obligations, and longer where additional tax or commercial-law obligations apply.
  • Audit logs: retained for 13 months by default; extendable on enterprise plans.
  • Support and communications: retained for three (3) years from the close of the most recent interaction, supporting long-running customer relationships and dispute resolution.
  • Marketing data: retained until you unsubscribe or object, after which we retain only a suppression record (your email address, hashed) to honour your opt-out.
  • Recruiting records: retained for two (2) years after the conclusion of the recruiting process unless you have consented to a longer period for inclusion in our talent pool.

At the end of the applicable retention period, personal data is deleted or anonymised. Where personal data is held in immutable backups that cannot be selectively erased, we apply technical and organisational measures (encryption, access restrictions) that effectively prevent the data from being accessed until the backup itself ages out of the retention window.

§ 11

Your rights

Depending on the jurisdiction whose law applies to you, you have the following rights with respect to your personal data. HelveX honours all rights set out below for all data subjects, regardless of jurisdiction, except where local law expressly excludes a particular right.

Right of access

You may obtain confirmation from us as to whether we process personal data about you and, where that is the case, access to that personal data along with the information specified in Article 15 GDPR (purposes, categories, recipients, retention, your other rights, the source of the data, and the existence of automated decision-making).

Right to rectification

You may obtain from us, without undue delay, the rectification of inaccurate personal data concerning you. You may also have incomplete personal data completed, including by means of providing a supplementary statement.

Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")

You may obtain the erasure of personal data concerning you in the circumstances set out in Article 17 GDPR (the data is no longer necessary, you withdraw consent, you object to processing based on legitimate interests and we have no overriding interest, the processing is unlawful, erasure is required for compliance with a legal obligation, or the data was collected in relation to information-society services offered to a child).

Right to data portability

For personal data that we process based on your consent or on a contract with you and that we process by automated means, you have the right to receive that personal data in a structured, commonly used and machine-readable format and to transmit it to another controller.

Right to restriction of processing

You may obtain the restriction of processing in the circumstances set out in Article 18 GDPR (accuracy is contested, processing is unlawful but you oppose erasure, we no longer need the data but you need it for legal claims, you have objected to processing pending verification of legitimate interests).

Right to object

You have the right to object on grounds relating to your particular situation to processing of personal data concerning you which is based on Article 6(1)(f) legitimate interests. We will no longer process the personal data unless we demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds for the processing that override your interests, rights, and freedoms, or for the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims. You may object to processing for direct marketing purposes at any time without justification; in that case we cease the marketing processing entirely.

Right not to be subject to automated decision-making

You have the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling, that produces legal effects concerning you or similarly significantly affects you, except where the decision is necessary for entering into or performing a contract, authorised by law with appropriate safeguards, or based on your explicit consent. See Section 13.

Right to withdraw consent

Where processing is based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.

How to exercise your rights

You may exercise any of your rights by contacting our Data Protection Officer at dpo@helvex.ai. We may need to verify your identity before responding to a request, both to protect you against impersonation and to comply with our own obligations. We aim to respond to substantive requests within thirty (30) days; where the request is complex or where we receive multiple requests, we may extend the period by two further months, with notice to you. We do not charge for responding to requests except where requests are manifestly unfounded or excessive, in which case we may charge a reasonable fee or refuse to act on the request.

Right to lodge a complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint with a data-protection supervisory authority. For Swiss-resident data subjects, the supervisory authority is the Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC), Feldeggweg 1, 3003 Berne. For EEA data subjects, you may lodge a complaint with the supervisory authority in your Member State of habitual residence, place of work, or place of the alleged infringement.

§ 13

Automated decision-making and profiling

Article 22 GDPR grants data subjects the right not to be subject to a decision based solely on automated processing, including profiling, which produces legal effects concerning them or similarly significantly affects them.

HelveX's direct use

HelveX itself does not use automated decision-making that produces legal effects on data subjects or similarly significantly affects them. We use automated systems for routine operational purposes (such as fraud detection during account sign-up, anti-abuse classification of model outputs, and routing of support requests), but these systems either operate at the platform-aggregate level or feed into human review before any decision that materially affects an individual is taken.

Customer use of the platform

Customers may build, on top of HelveX, systems that use AI-generated outputs to support decisions affecting their own users, employees, or other data subjects. Where those decisions are based solely on automated processing and produce legal effects or similarly significantly affect individuals, the Customer (as controller) is required to comply with Article 22 GDPR, including by obtaining a lawful basis for the automated decision-making, by implementing suitable measures to safeguard data subjects' rights, by enabling human intervention, and by disclosing the use to data subjects. HelveX provides technical capabilities (model cards, eval data, output classifications, audit logging) to support Customers in meeting these obligations; HelveX does not, however, assume responsibility for the Customer's compliance.

EU AI Act

Where the Customer's use of HelveX constitutes the deployment of a high-risk AI system under Annex III of the EU AI Act, the Customer assumes the obligations of a deployer. Where the use constitutes a prohibited AI practice under Article 5 of the AI Act, the Customer is in breach of the Acceptable Use Policy in addition to the AI Act. See the Trust Center, Section 12.

§ 14

Security

HelveX implements appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect personal data against unauthorised or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, or damage. Our security programme is described in detail on the Security page and operationalised through controls evaluated annually under SOC 2 Type II and ISO/IEC 27001:2022.

At a high level, the measures include: AES-256 encryption at rest with HSM-backed key management; TLS 1.3 encryption in transit; multi-factor authentication for all staff and recommended for customers; just-in-time elevation for production access; least-privilege role-based access control with quarterly access reviews; continuous vulnerability management; annual third-party penetration testing; 24/7 monitoring and incident response; and disaster-recovery capabilities meeting an RPO of one hour and an RTO of four hours at the platform control-plane level.

No security programme can eliminate all risk. We commit to notify Customers of confirmed Personal Data Breaches affecting their Customer Content without undue delay and in any event within 72 hours of confirmation, as required by GDPR Article 33 and operationalised in our incident-response programme.
