Legal · Privacy

Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how HelveX AG ("HelveX", "we", "us") collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal data when a Customer uses the HelveX AI builder platform to design, generate, edit, deploy, and operate web applications, marketing sites, internal tools, API backends, and other digital platforms — and when the End Users of those built applications interact with them. The Policy is harmonised with the GDPR (EU/EEA), the Swiss FADP / nFADP, the UK GDPR, and the CCPA / CPRA (California).

EffectiveMay 2026 Last updatedMay 2026 Version1.0 Governing lawSwitzerland
§ 01

Scope and applicability

This Privacy Policy applies to all personal data that HelveX AG processes in connection with the HelveX platform. The platform is an AI-assisted builder: a Customer describes, in natural language, the application, website, dashboard, API backend, or digital platform they wish to create, and HelveX generates the underlying code, configuration, design artefacts, and deployment manifests required to produce that result. The Customer can then edit those artefacts, connect databases and third-party services, deploy the resulting application on HelveX-operated hosting infrastructure or export it to a cloud target of their choice, invite collaborators, version their work, and ultimately make the application available to End Users.

This Policy applies to every surface of that lifecycle: the public website at helvex.ai, the authenticated Builder environment, the AI generation and refinement pipelines, the integrated code editor, the preview and sandbox runtimes, the deployment infrastructure on which Generated Applications run, the public and private APIs, the command-line tooling and SDKs, the customer support channels, the billing systems, and the surrounding marketing, documentation, and corporate surface. It applies regardless of whether the Customer accesses these services from a desktop browser, a mobile device, an embedded extension, a programmatic API client, or via a partner integration.

The Policy uses three operative terms in a precise way and the reader should keep them distinct throughout. The "Customer" is the account holder — the natural or legal person who has registered with HelveX, accepted the Terms of Service, and uses the Builder to design and deploy applications. "User Content" means everything the Customer provides to HelveX (prompts, instructions, uploaded files, design references, source code authored by the Customer, configuration data, credentials supplied for integrations) together with everything that HelveX generates on the Customer's behalf in response (AI Output, including generated code, designs, copy, configuration files, deployment artefacts, and intermediate reasoning traces where retained). An "End User" is any natural person who interacts with an application that a Customer has built and deployed using HelveX — for example, a visitor to a marketing site generated on HelveX, an authenticated user of a SaaS dashboard built and hosted on HelveX, or a participant in a marketplace platform that a Customer has launched on HelveX.

The relationship between Customer, User Content, and End User is the structural backbone of this Policy. With respect to data that the Customer provides directly to HelveX or that HelveX collects directly about the Customer's use of the Builder, HelveX acts as the controller and this Privacy Policy is the principal instrument. With respect to personal data of End Users that flows through a Customer's Generated Application — for example, registrations, profiles, transactions, messages, uploads, or analytics events collected by the Customer's app — the Customer is the controller and HelveX acts as the Customer's processor. The processor relationship is governed by the Data Processing Addendum (the "DPA") available at /dpa, which is incorporated by reference into the Customer's agreement with HelveX.

This Policy does not cover the privacy practices of third parties whose websites or services may be linked from helvex.ai, integrated into a Generated Application at the Customer's direction, or otherwise accessed from within the Builder. When the Customer authorises an integration with a third-party service — such as a payment processor, an authentication identity provider, an analytics service, or a third-party API — that third party's own privacy notice governs the data exchanged with the third party.

§ 03

Information we collect from Customers

HelveX collects personal data about Customers along three principal axes: account information that the Customer provides to register and operate an account; Builder Inputs that the Customer submits when using the platform; and service-generated telemetry that HelveX captures automatically when the Customer interacts with the Services. Each axis is described below in detail.

3.1 Account information

When a Customer registers for an account, signs an Order Form, or otherwise enters into a contractual relationship with HelveX, HelveX collects and processes the following categories of account information: the Customer's full name; the Customer's primary work or personal email address; the name and (where applicable) registered legal form of the organisation, workspace, or tenant with which the Customer is associated; the Customer's role or job title where it has been provided; the Customer's authentication identifiers, including a password (stored solely as a cryptographically hashed value computed with a modern key-derivation function), single-sign-on subject identifiers, multi-factor authentication enrolment metadata, API keys, and personal access tokens; billing details, including the billing contact's name and email, the billing address, the tax identifier (VAT or equivalent where applicable), and a tokenised reference to the payment instrument held by HelveX's payment processor (HelveX never stores raw card data); and the contents of any support correspondence, including the body of email and ticket messages, screenshots and attachments the Customer chooses to attach, and the diagnostic data the Customer elects to share in connection with a support request.

3.2 Builder Inputs

"Builder Inputs" means the materials that the Customer submits to HelveX in the course of designing, generating, editing, and deploying an application. Builder Inputs include, without limitation: natural-language prompts and instructions submitted to the AI generation pipeline; design requirements, mockups, wireframes, and reference materials uploaded by the Customer; code, configuration, scripts, and documentation that the Customer authors directly within the Builder or pastes into chat interfaces; data files, sample records, and seed datasets uploaded by the Customer to support generation, testing, or migration; deployment-target settings, including the cloud region, scaling parameters, and feature-flag selections chosen by the Customer; environment variables, configuration secrets, and the credentials necessary to connect Generated Applications to databases, third-party APIs, identity providers, payment processors, or other services; OAuth authorisation grants for third-party integrations the Customer connects; and any other content the Customer chooses to provide to the Builder in the course of their work.

To the extent any Builder Input contains personal data — whether of the Customer, of the Customer's colleagues, or of any third party — HelveX processes that personal data as the Customer's processor, on the Customer's documented instructions, and subject to the DPA. The Customer is responsible for ensuring it has a lawful basis to submit any personal data to the Builder and for honouring its disclosure obligations to the data subjects concerned.

3.3 Service-generated telemetry

HelveX automatically collects telemetry generated by the Customer's interaction with the Services. Telemetry includes: build logs, including the steps executed during a generation or deployment pipeline, the inputs at each step, and the outcome; deployment logs, including the artefacts deployed, the timestamps of deployment events, and the deployment-target metadata; error traces and stack traces emitted by the Builder, the preview environment, or a Generated Application running on HelveX infrastructure; runtime metrics for hosted Generated Applications, including request counts, latencies, CPU and memory usage, and error rates, sampled in aggregate; browser session data when the Customer uses the Builder UI, including device type and operating-system class, screen dimensions, browser identifier and version, language preference, and approximate geolocation derived from IP address; the IP address from which a Customer connects, the user-agent string of the client used to connect, the time and duration of each session, the navigation paths within the Builder, and similar interaction events; and audit trails of administrative actions taken by the Customer or by the Customer's authorised collaborators within the Customer's workspace, including invitations, permission changes, billing-plan changes, key rotations, and configuration updates.

Telemetry is used by HelveX as a controller for the legitimate-interest purposes set out in §7 (service delivery, security, abuse detection, billing reconciliation, and product improvement) and is not used to train HelveX-developed base models on Customer Content.

§ 04

AI-generated outputs — handling and treatment

AI-generated outputs ("AI Output") are central to the Service and therefore receive dedicated treatment in this Policy. AI Output includes generated source code, configuration files, infrastructure-as-code manifests, generated text and copy, generated designs and design specifications, generated structured data, conversation responses, classification and routing decisions, embeddings, and intermediate reasoning traces where retained. AI Output is generated in response to Builder Inputs that the Customer submits.

AI Output is part of User Content. The Customer retains ownership of AI Output to the maximum extent permitted by applicable law, subject to the licence to HelveX described in the Terms of Service that is necessary to operate the Services. AI Output is stored within the Customer's project workspace, is subject to the same access controls as the Customer's other content, and is included in the Customer's right to export and delete their data.

HelveX does not use Customer prompts or AI-generated outputs to train HelveX-developed base models. Customer Content is not contributed to HelveX-controlled training corpora by default. Any future change to this position would require an explicit opt-in by the Customer, would be documented in a clearly identified product setting, and would apply only on a forward-looking basis.

Where HelveX uses third-party foundation model providers to produce AI Output — that is, where the Customer's prompt is routed to an external model maintained by a third party rather than being processed exclusively by a model controlled by HelveX — HelveX selects providers that offer contractual no-training defaults for inference traffic and transmits a no-training flag in the generation request where the provider supports it. The current list of foundation model providers acting as Sub-processors is available to Customers in Annex III of the DPA under MNDA via trust@helvex.ai; the categorical description is included in §9 below.

AI Output is a statistical artefact: it is produced by models that observed large training corpora during their development, and the patterns surfaced in any given output may resemble patterns present in those corpora. HelveX makes no representation that AI Output is original, non-infringing, accurate, complete, free of vulnerabilities, or fit for the Customer's particular purpose. The Customer is the sole party responsible for verifying that any AI Output the Customer deploys, distributes, or otherwise relies upon: (a) is compatible with the licences of any open-source software the output resembles or incorporates; (b) is free of security vulnerabilities the Customer cannot accept; (c) does not infringe any third party's intellectual-property or other rights; and (d) is suitable for the Customer's intended use. HelveX provides best-effort tooling for attribution, licence detection, and vulnerability scanning where such tooling is available within the Builder, but no such tooling is exhaustive and none discharges the Customer's responsibility.

AI Output may not be suitable, without independent expert review, for safety-critical contexts including but not limited to medical diagnosis, automated treatment selection, life-support systems, aircraft, motor-vehicle control, nuclear-facility operation, regulated financial advice, regulated investment recommendations, legal advice with legally significant effect, and high-stakes decision-making affecting individuals' fundamental rights. The Customer warrants that it will not deploy AI Output into such contexts without the human review, professional sign-off, and validation procedures the relevant domain requires.

§ 05

End User data flowing through hosted applications

When a Customer builds a Generated Application and deploys it on HelveX Hosted Services (or, where applicable, deploys it to the Customer's own cloud target while continuing to use HelveX-operated platform features such as authentication, telemetry, or storage), End Users will typically interact with that application and may provide personal data to it. Such personal data may include registration information, profile attributes, transaction records, uploaded content, in-application messages, location data, device identifiers, payment-related identifiers tokenised through a payment processor, and any other information the End User submits to or generates within the Customer's application.

For all such End User personal data, the Customer is the Controller within the meaning of the GDPR, the Swiss FADP / nFADP, the UK GDPR, and substantially equivalent regimes. HelveX, in providing the underlying hosting, storage, networking, and platform services that enable the Customer's application to operate, acts as the Customer's Processor. The terms governing this processor relationship are set out in the Data Processing Addendum at /dpa, which is incorporated by reference into the Customer's agreement with HelveX and which addresses the matters required by Article 28 GDPR.

HelveX does not access or use End User personal data flowing through hosted Generated Applications for any purpose other than (i) providing the hosting and supporting services on the Customer's instructions; (ii) maintaining the security, integrity, and availability of the platform on which the Customer's application runs; (iii) responding to lawful binding requests from competent authorities; and (iv) where strictly necessary, investigating violations of the Acceptable Use Policy or this Policy. HelveX does not, in particular, mine End User data to train HelveX-developed base models, build marketing profiles of End Users, monetise End User data, or otherwise process End User data for HelveX's independent commercial purposes.

End Users who wish to exercise their data-subject rights with respect to data held in a Generated Application built by a Customer should direct their request to the Customer in the first instance. HelveX will refer End User requests received directly back to the Customer and will provide the Customer with reasonable technical assistance to respond, in accordance with the DPA.

§ 06

Cookies and similar technologies

HelveX uses cookies, local storage, session storage, and similar browser technologies on its public website and within the authenticated Builder. The technologies are grouped into three categories.

6.1 Strictly necessary

Strictly necessary cookies and storage entries are required for the basic operation of the website and the Builder. They include session-state cookies that maintain the Customer's authenticated session between requests; cross-site request forgery (CSRF) tokens that mitigate request-forgery attacks; identifiers used to associate the Customer with their billing state and entitlement; and short-lived cookies used to implement load-balancing and session affinity. These cookies cannot be disabled because doing so would render the Services non-functional.

6.2 Preferences

Preferences storage records the Customer's choices about how the user interface presents information — for example, the chosen language for the Builder UI, the chosen theme (light, dark, or system), the chosen units of measurement, the visibility of optional onboarding hints, and the layout configuration of editor panels. Preferences storage is stored under the Customer's profile where the Customer is signed in, and in local-storage entries on the device otherwise.

6.3 Analytics

Analytics technologies record aggregate, first-party measurements of how the website and the Builder are used: which pages are visited, in what order, which features are exercised, where users encounter errors, and how long sessions last. HelveX uses analytics exclusively for first-party product-improvement purposes and does not place any third-party advertising cookies on Customer or End User devices via the HelveX surface. HelveX does not engage in cross-context behavioural advertising as defined under the CPRA, does not sell Personal Data, and does not share Personal Data for cross-context behavioural advertising.

The Customer can disable optional categories (preferences and analytics) through the privacy-preferences panel available from the website footer and from within the Builder. The strictly-necessary category cannot be disabled.

§ 07

How we use Customer information

HelveX uses Customer information for the purposes set out below. Each purpose is associated with a category of personal data and a legal basis under §8.

7.1 Service delivery

HelveX uses account information, Builder Inputs, AI Output, and service-generated telemetry to provide the Services: to authenticate the Customer; to run the AI generation and editing pipeline; to host previews and Generated Applications; to apply the Customer's configuration; to deliver requested integrations; to surface status and logs back to the Customer; to enable collaboration with the Customer's invited teammates; and to maintain the Customer's session.

7.2 Billing and contract management

HelveX uses account information, billing details, and usage metrics to invoice the Customer, to reconcile payments, to manage renewals and overage, to apply credits or discounts, to issue receipts, to comply with tax obligations including the Swiss accounting-retention obligations referred to in §11, and to administer the contractual relationship with the Customer.

7.3 Support

HelveX uses support correspondence, screenshots and attachments the Customer chooses to provide, diagnostic data, and related telemetry to respond to Customer support requests, to diagnose incidents the Customer reports, to escalate complex issues internally and where necessary to relevant Sub-processors, and to follow up after resolution to confirm the Customer's satisfaction.

7.4 Security and abuse prevention

HelveX uses authentication identifiers, IP address and user-agent telemetry, behavioural signals derived from Builder usage, and content classifiers operating on Builder Inputs and AI Output to detect, prevent, investigate, and respond to: unauthorised access; account take-over attempts; credential stuffing; brute-force attacks; bot abuse; rate-limit evasion; mass-scale jailbreak attempts; generation of content prohibited under the Acceptable Use Policy; and other malicious or fraudulent behaviour.

7.5 AUP enforcement

Where HelveX has a reasonable basis to believe a Customer or End User of a Generated Application is violating the Acceptable Use Policy, HelveX may review the relevant Builder Inputs, AI Output, deployed application configuration, and content surfaced to End Users for the limited purpose of investigating the suspected violation, taking enforcement action (which may include warning, suspending the affected feature, suspending the Account, or terminating the Account in cases of severe or repeated violations), responding to abuse reports, responding to DMCA / copyright complaints, and reporting unlawful content to competent authorities where required by law.

7.6 Product improvement

HelveX uses aggregated, de-identified telemetry — counts, rates, distributions, and similar measures that cannot reasonably identify any individual Customer or End User — to improve the Services: to prioritise engineering work, to test infrastructure changes, to evaluate the performance and quality of HelveX-developed models in aggregate, and to surface feature opportunities. HelveX does not use Customer Content (Builder Inputs or AI Output) to train HelveX-developed base models; product-improvement work proceeds either from synthetic data or, where Customer telemetry is involved, from telemetry from which content has been excluded.

7.7 Compliance with legal obligations

HelveX uses Customer information to comply with applicable legal obligations, including responding to lawful binding requests from courts, regulators, and competent authorities; meeting tax, accounting, and corporate-record obligations; honouring obligations under the GDPR, the Swiss FADP / nFADP, the UK GDPR, the CCPA / CPRA, and similar regimes; and meeting obligations under the EU AI Act, the Digital Services Act, and equivalent emerging regulation as applicable.

7.8 Communication

HelveX uses Customer contact information to send transactional communications relating to the Services — account notifications, security alerts, billing statements, sub-processor change notices, planned-maintenance notices, incident updates, end-of-life notices, and material changes to legal documents — and, with the Customer's consent or on a legitimate-interest basis subject to the right to object, occasional product and educational communications relevant to the Customer's role.

§ 08

Legal bases for processing

For Customers and other data subjects to whom the GDPR, the Swiss FADP / nFADP, or the UK GDPR apply, HelveX processes personal data on the following legal bases.

Performance of a contract (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR)
Processing necessary to deliver the Services to the Customer, including account registration and authentication; provisioning of the Builder and Hosted Services; generation, storage, and delivery of AI Output; deployment and operation of Generated Applications; processing of Customer support requests; and billing and contract administration.
Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR)
Processing necessary for HelveX's legitimate interests where those interests are not overridden by the data subject's rights and freedoms. Such interests include securing the Services against unauthorised access and abuse; detecting and responding to AUP violations; managing fraud and credit risk; producing aggregated product analytics; preventing material disruption to other Customers; protecting HelveX's intellectual property; and supporting normal business administration (corporate planning, financial management, recruitment, audit). A balancing test is documented for each material legitimate-interest processing activity.
Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR)
Where HelveX relies on the Customer's consent — for example, for non-essential cookies, for optional marketing communications, for participation in beta programmes that involve additional processing, or for processing that requires explicit consent under applicable law — HelveX seeks that consent before commencing the processing. Consent may be withdrawn at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before the withdrawal.
Compliance with a legal obligation (Art. 6(1)(c) GDPR)
Processing necessary to comply with applicable legal obligations imposed on HelveX, including tax and accounting retention, response to lawful binding requests from competent authorities, sanctions and export-control screening where applicable, and statutory record-keeping.
Protection of vital interests (Art. 6(1)(d) GDPR)
Where strictly necessary, to protect the vital interests of a data subject or of another natural person — for example, in response to a credible threat-to-life signal identified within the Services. HelveX relies on this basis only as a matter of exception.

Where HelveX acts as the Customer's processor with respect to End User personal data flowing through a Generated Application, the Customer is responsible for identifying the appropriate legal basis for the processing of End User data and for honouring its obligations to End Users. HelveX processes such data on the Customer's documented instructions and subject to the DPA.

§ 09

Sharing and disclosure

9.1 Sub-processors

HelveX engages a limited set of Sub-processors to support the operation of the Services. Sub-processors operate under written contracts that impose data-protection obligations no less protective than those HelveX owes to its Customers. The categorical disclosure is as follows: cloud infrastructure providers (Amazon Web Services and Google Cloud Platform are HelveX's primary cloud providers; Microsoft Azure is available on opt-in for enterprise Customers with specific regional or sovereignty requirements); third-party foundation model providers, used to produce AI Output where Customer prompts are routed to an external model rather than to a HelveX-controlled model; transactional email providers, used to deliver authentication, billing, security, and support emails; payment processors, used to authorise and capture card and bank payments and to manage subscription billing (HelveX uses Stripe for card processing); error-monitoring providers, used to capture and aggregate error traces and runtime exceptions; customer-support tooling vendors, used to manage support tickets and Customer correspondence; and limited categorical analytics vendors as described in §6.

The full current list of Sub-processors, including provider identities, the regions in which they process data, and the specific functions for which each is engaged, is published as Annex III of the DPA available to authorised Customer representatives under MNDA via trust@helvex.ai. HelveX provides at least thirty (30) days' advance notice before adding a new Sub-processor in a material category or replacing one of HelveX's primary cloud or foundation-model providers; the notice is delivered to the account-billing contact and posted to /trust-center. The Customer has the right to object on reasonable grounds; the procedure for objections is set out in §6 of the DPA.

9.2 Legal compliance

HelveX may disclose personal data when required to do so by applicable law, including in response to court orders, subpoenas, search warrants, and lawful binding demands from regulators or law-enforcement authorities. HelveX evaluates each demand for legal validity and scope, narrows disclosures to what is required, challenges demands that are facially invalid or overbroad where reasonably possible, and (where lawfully permitted and not under a non-disclosure order) notifies the affected Customer before disclosing the Customer's data so that the Customer can take action to protect its interests. HelveX intends to publish an annual transparency report summarising the volume and nature of legal requests received in the preceding year.

9.3 Business transfers

If HelveX is involved in a merger, acquisition, sale of substantially all of its assets, financing, reorganisation, or similar transaction, personal data may be disclosed to the prospective counterparty and subsequently transferred to the successor entity as part of the transaction. HelveX will require any such counterparty or successor to honour the commitments made in this Policy with respect to personal data transferred and will notify Customers of any material change to the controller of their data resulting from such a transaction.

9.4 With Customer consent

HelveX may share personal data in other circumstances with the Customer's prior consent, including where the Customer instructs HelveX to share data with a specific third party.

9.5 No sale, no behavioural-advertising sharing

HelveX does not sell Personal Data and does not share Personal Data for cross-context behavioural advertising, as those terms are defined under the CCPA / CPRA and substantially equivalent regimes. HelveX has not sold Personal Data in the preceding twelve (12) months and has no current plans to do so.

§ 10

International transfers

HelveX is headquartered in Switzerland and primary processing of Customer Content and Generated Application data takes place in HelveX's EU-Central region (Frankfurt) and HelveX's CH-Central region (Zurich), with intra-EEA and intra-EFTA replication for resilience. Where the Customer has specifically requested a different processing region, HelveX provisions the Customer's resources in that region; the currently available regions are published in HelveX's documentation.

Where personal data is transferred from the EEA, Switzerland, or the United Kingdom to a country that has not been the subject of an adequacy decision by the European Commission, the Swiss Federal Council, or the UK competent authority respectively, HelveX implements safeguards required by applicable law. In particular, HelveX relies on the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses (Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914 of 4 June 2021) ("SCCs") in Module 2 (Controller to Processor) and Module 3 (Processor to Sub-processor) configurations as applicable, supplemented by the Swiss Addendum published by the Swiss FDPIC where Swiss-origin data is transferred, and by the UK International Data Transfer Addendum (IDTA) where UK-origin data is transferred.

HelveX layers technical and organisational supplementary measures over the contractual measures to address the case-law concerns underlying the Schrems II decision and the corresponding Swiss FDPIC and UK ICO guidance. Supplementary measures include: encryption of personal data in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256-GCM); access controls implementing least-privilege and multi-factor authentication for personnel with access to personal data; logging of administrative access for forensic review; data-minimisation in cross-border flows so that only the data strictly necessary for the relevant Sub-processor function is transferred; contractual no-training defaults with foundation-model providers as described in §4 and §18; and, on enterprise terms, optional regional pinning that restricts processing to a Customer-nominated region for the lifecycle of the Customer's data. Transfer Impact Assessments documenting these measures by category of recipient are available to authorised Customer representatives under MNDA via trust@helvex.ai.

§ 11

Retention

HelveX retains personal data only for as long as is necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, accounting, tax, or reporting requirements imposed on HelveX. The principal retention rules are set out below.

Account data
Retained for the lifetime of the Account plus thirty (30) days following Account closure. Reasonable additional retention may apply to a narrow subset of fields required for chargeback and dispute resolution.
User Content — Customer projects, code, designs, configurations, AI Output
Retained for the lifetime of the Customer's subscription, plus a ninety (90)-day grace period following subscription termination during which the Customer may export their content. After the grace period, User Content is deleted from primary storage; residual copies persist only in encrypted backups subject to the rolling backup-retention schedule below.
Deployed Generated Applications and their state
Retained until the Customer deletes the application or the Account is terminated. A thirty (30)-day soft-delete grace applies after a Customer-initiated deletion, during which restoration is possible on Customer request. After the grace period, the application and its associated state are deleted from primary storage.
End User personal data within hosted Generated Applications
Retained according to the Customer's documented instructions and the configuration the Customer chooses for its application. Per the DPA, HelveX returns or deletes End User personal data on the Customer's instructions and at termination.
Billing and accounting records
Retained for the period required by the Swiss Code of Obligations (Art. 958f), which is ten (10) years for accounting documents, and by equivalent obligations in other jurisdictions where applicable.
Service logs and telemetry
Retained for thirteen (13) months by default; enterprise Customers may configure log retention down to thirty (30) days, subject to operational and security minima. Logs required for an active security investigation or active litigation hold are preserved for the duration of the matter.
Encrypted backups
Rotated on a schedule that does not exceed ninety (90) days; backup copies of deleted Customer Content are purged from backup media within ninety (90) days of the deletion in primary storage.
Support correspondence
Retained for three (3) years following case closure for quality-assurance, dispute-resolution, and training purposes.
§ 12

Customer rights

Where the GDPR, the Swiss FADP / nFADP, the UK GDPR, or substantially equivalent regimes apply, Customers and other data subjects have the following rights with respect to personal data that HelveX processes about them as a controller. Each right is exercised by writing to dpo@helvex.ai.

Right of access
The right to obtain confirmation as to whether HelveX is processing the data subject's personal data and, where it is, a copy of the personal data and information about the processing.
Right to rectification
The right to have inaccurate personal data corrected without undue delay and to have incomplete personal data completed.
Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
The right to have personal data erased without undue delay where the data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected; where the data subject has withdrawn consent and there is no other legal basis for the processing; where the data subject objects and there are no overriding legitimate grounds; where the data has been unlawfully processed; or where erasure is required by law. Erasure does not apply to data HelveX is required to retain by law or to data necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims.
Right to data portability
The right to receive personal data the data subject has provided to HelveX, in a structured, commonly-used, machine-readable format, and to transmit that data to another controller where the processing is based on consent or on contract and is carried out by automated means.
Right to restriction of processing
The right to restrict processing in defined circumstances — for example, while the accuracy of the data is being verified, or while an objection is being considered.
Right to object
The right to object, on grounds relating to the data subject's particular situation, to processing carried out on the basis of legitimate interests; HelveX will discontinue the processing unless compelling legitimate grounds override the data subject's interests or the processing is necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims.
Right to lodge a complaint
The right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority — see §21.
Right not to be subject to automated decision-making with legal or similarly significant effect
See §17.
Right to withdraw consent
Where processing is based on consent, the right to withdraw consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing carried out before the withdrawal.

HelveX responds to verified data-subject requests within thirty (30) days of receipt. § 18

AI inputs and outputs — additional disclosures

This section restates and clarifies HelveX's commitments regarding AI inputs (prompts) and AI outputs, building on the framework established in §4.

Customer prompts submitted to the Builder are transmitted to the AI generation pipeline solely for the purpose of fulfilling the generation request. Where the pipeline routes the prompt to a third-party foundation model provider (acting as a Sub-processor for the request), the prompt is transmitted to that provider under a contract that requires the provider not to use the prompt or the corresponding output to train any model and that requires the provider to delete the prompt and output from inference systems after the request is served, subject only to short-term operational logs.

AI outputs are returned to the Customer's Project workspace and stored as Customer Content, subject to the Customer's access controls and to the retention rules in §11. AI outputs are not contributed to HelveX-controlled training corpora, are not used to train HelveX-developed base models, and are not transmitted to third-party model providers for training. HelveX does not retain prompts or outputs at the model-provider layer beyond what is needed to deliver and log the request.

Enterprise Customers may, on enterprise terms, enable a zero-retention mode in which prompts and outputs are not persisted to HelveX-controlled service logs beyond the lifecycle of the request. In zero-retention mode, generation logs retain only metadata necessary for billing reconciliation, abuse detection, and incident response (request identifier, account identifier, model identifier, token counts, latency, success/failure flag, classifier outcomes) and exclude the prompt content and the output content themselves. Zero-retention mode does not change the storage of outputs that the Customer chooses to keep within their Project workspace.

HelveX surfaces, within the Builder, telemetry sufficient for the Customer to audit prompt routing decisions made by the pipeline. The Customer can, on enterprise terms, restrict generation routing to a named subset of approved foundation-model providers ("approved-providers-only" mode).

§ 19

Security

HelveX maintains a comprehensive security programme described in full at /security-page. A high-level summary is provided here.

HelveX encrypts personal data in transit using TLS 1.3 and at rest using AES-256-GCM with HSM-backed key management. Access to personal data within HelveX is governed by role-based access controls that implement least-privilege defaults, with mandatory multi-factor authentication for HelveX personnel and strongly-recommended multi-factor authentication for Customer accounts. HelveX implements logical isolation between Customer projects and between Generated Applications, with dedicated-infrastructure options available on enterprise terms. HelveX runs Generated Application previews and certain code-execution operations inside sandboxed environments with no privileged host access, strict resource limits, ephemeral filesystems, and restricted network egress.

HelveX monitors the platform with a centralised SIEM, applies anomaly detection to identify suspicious behaviour, conducts internal scanning of infrastructure and application code on a defined cadence, and commissions third-party penetration testing annually and on material architecture changes. HelveX operates an incident-response programme with severity-classified escalation paths, a 24/7 on-call rotation, and a Customer-notification commitment of seventy-two (72) hours from confirmation for a Personal Data Breach affecting Customer data, aligned with Article 33 GDPR.

HelveX maintains business-continuity and disaster-recovery capabilities including geo-replicated backups, point-in-time recovery, multi-region failover for the platform control plane, and quarterly DR exercises with an annual full-failover test.

The full description, including the security framework alignment, the AI-specific controls (prompt-injection defences, output safety filtering, sandboxing of Generated Application previews, model-supply-chain integrity), and the shared-responsibility model with the Customer, is set out at /security-page.
