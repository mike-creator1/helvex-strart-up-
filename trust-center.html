Trust Center

HelveX is built for organisations that take security, privacy, and AI governance seriously. This page consolidates the policies, certifications, and operational disclosures we make available to customers, regulators, and prospective partners.

§ 01

Trust framework

Trust is the product of consistent practice over time, not a marketing claim. HelveX AG operates a trust framework built around five guiding principles that govern every architectural, operational, and contractual decision we make. These principles are documented internally as binding policy and externalised here so that customers, auditors, and regulators can verify them against our behaviour.

The five principles

The first principle is defense in depth. No single control protects the platform. We layer perimeter controls, network segmentation, identity-based authorisation, application hardening, and continuous monitoring so that a failure in any one layer is contained by the layers beneath and above it. Defense in depth is what allows us to absorb novel threats without losing the entire perimeter.

The second principle is least privilege. Every identity in the platform, whether human or service, is granted only the permissions strictly required to perform its function. Access expands by exception and by ticket, never by default. Privileged operations on production systems require explicit, time-bound elevation that is logged, reviewed, and revoked automatically when the window closes.

The third principle is encryption everywhere. Customer Content is encrypted in transit and at rest using modern, industry-standard algorithms. Keys are managed in hardware-backed key management services with rotation policies that exceed regulatory minimums. We do not treat encryption as an option that can be disabled for performance or convenience.

The fourth principle is replayable audit. Every consequential action in the platform, including prompt submissions, model completions, tool invocations, data access events, and administrative changes, is logged with actor identity, timestamp, and outcome. Audit records are immutable from the perspective of the application tier, retained for a defined period, and exportable to customer-controlled systems for independent verification.

The fifth principle is the your-cloud option. For customers whose risk model requires that Customer Content never leave their own cloud tenancy, HelveX offers deployment of the platform inside the customer's own AWS or GCP environment. The customer retains full control of the underlying infrastructure, network perimeter, and encryption keys; HelveX operates the platform under a managed-services arrangement.

§ 02

Certifications and frameworks

HelveX maintains an active certification programme covering the platform, the workspace surfaces, and the managed engagement practice. We have intentionally chosen depth over breadth: each certification we hold reflects an annual independent audit, not a one-time exercise, and we publish surveillance results to customers under NDA.

SOC 2 Type II

HelveX undergoes an annual SOC 2 Type II examination performed by an independent firm of certified public accountants. The examination evaluates the design and operating effectiveness of our controls against the AICPA Trust Services Criteria across the Security, Availability, and Confidentiality categories over a continuous twelve-month observation period. SOC 2 Type II is the appropriate framework for evaluating a cloud-services provider's operational rigour over time, distinct from SOC 2 Type I which evaluates a point-in-time design only.

ISO/IEC 27001:2022

HelveX is certified to ISO/IEC 27001:2022, the international standard for information security management systems. The certificate covers the scope of HelveX's platform operations, including the three production models (Ether, Nexus, Prometheus), the workspace, and the corporate environment supporting those services. Certification entails the establishment, implementation, maintenance, and continual improvement of an information security management system, with annual surveillance audits and a recertification cycle every three years.

GDPR, Swiss FADP / nFADP

HelveX is aligned with the General Data Protection Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2016/679, GDPR) for European Economic Area customers, the United Kingdom General Data Protection Regulation for UK customers, and the revised Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (nFADP, in force from 1 September 2023) for Swiss-resident data. Alignment is operationalised through our Data Processing Addendum, our internal records of processing activities, our impact assessment processes, and our standard offering of European Commission Standard Contractual Clauses for international transfers where adequacy decisions do not apply.

Report availability

Customers and prospective customers who have executed a mutual non-disclosure agreement may request copies of our current SOC 2 Type II report, our ISO 27001 certificate and statement of applicability, and our most recent independent penetration test summary by writing to trust@helvex.ai. We respond to substantive trust-package requests within ten business days. We do not publish certification artifacts publicly because they contain detailed control descriptions that, when read in isolation by adversaries, could inform targeted attacks; the MNDA-gated channel preserves the integrity of the audit information.

§ 03

Compliance scope

Customers reviewing HelveX during procurement reasonably ask: which parts of the offering are inside the certification perimeter, and which sit outside? This section answers that question precisely so that no compliance representation is left ambiguous.

Products in scope

The following are within the scope of our SOC 2 Type II examination and our ISO/IEC 27001:2022 certification: model inference for Ether (the real-time tier), Nexus (the balanced reasoning tier, longest context window in the family), and Prometheus (the frontier reasoning tier, including agentic capabilities); the workspace surfaces, comprising Chat, Projects, Agents, Knowledge, Spaces, and History; the underlying control plane, observability stack, identity provider, and authentication subsystem; and the corporate security programme supporting those services.

Engagements in scope

Managed engagements delivered by HelveX's senior-engineer practice are conducted under the same information-security management system as the platform. Engagement engineers operate from the same hardened workstation baseline, with the same identity and access controls, the same data-handling policies, and the same audit-logging discipline. When an engagement requires access to Customer Content or Customer systems, that access is provisioned under written instruction, time-bound, and logged.

Regions in scope

HelveX's primary production environment runs in EU-Central regions (specifically eu-central-1 on AWS, with europe-west6 (Zurich) on GCP for selected workloads). Secondary regions in North America and Asia-Pacific are available on request for enterprise deployments. All production regions are within the scope of certification. Development and staging environments are operationally segregated from production and are subject to a separate, equally rigorous control regime.

Out of scope

Public-facing marketing properties, including the helvex.ai website itself, the press kit, and ungated documentation, are operated under a lighter control baseline that does not handle Customer Content. They are nevertheless covered by our standard application-security and vulnerability-management programmes.

§ 04

Security architecture overview

The HelveX platform is designed around the assumption that any single layer can be compromised and the system must continue to protect Customer Content. The architecture below describes the controls in place at each layer.

Encryption

All Customer Content is encrypted at rest using AES-256 in Galois/Counter Mode (AES-256-GCM) for object storage and for database volumes. Encryption keys are managed in cloud-provider hardware security modules (AWS KMS with FIPS 140-2 Level 3 validated HSMs, GCP Cloud KMS with comparable assurances). Customer-managed encryption keys (BYOK) are available for enterprise tenants who require cryptographic separation from HelveX-managed key material; in that configuration, HelveX cannot decrypt Customer Content if the customer revokes our principal's key access.

All network traffic terminating at HelveX endpoints is encrypted using TLS 1.3 with modern cipher suites; TLS 1.2 is supported only for legacy clients and is deprecated. Certificate pinning is enforced on internal service-to-service communications. We do not accept connections using deprecated TLS versions or null ciphers.

Tenant isolation

In our multi-tenant production environment, tenant isolation is enforced logically at three distinct layers: at the identity layer through scoped credentials that cannot cross tenant boundaries; at the application layer through tenant-id checks embedded in every data access path and verified by automated policy tests in our continuous-integration pipeline; and at the data layer through tenant-scoped partitioning and row-level security on shared datastores. For enterprise customers who require physical separation, HelveX offers single-tenant deployments in dedicated infrastructure, with per-tenant encryption keys, dedicated network segments, and isolated identity stores.

Key management

Encryption keys follow a defined lifecycle: generation in HSM, distribution through audited channels, use within constrained service principals, rotation on a 90-day schedule (or sooner for incident-driven rotation), and cryptographic destruction at end-of-life. Master keys are never exported in plaintext from the HSM boundary. Customer-managed keys are accessed via cross-account assume-role patterns that can be severed unilaterally by the customer.

Build and supply chain

All HelveX-produced software artifacts are built in hermetic build environments and signed with cryptographic build attestations. Third-party dependencies are evaluated against our software composition analysis pipeline before introduction and re-evaluated continuously thereafter. Container base images are minimal, hardened, and patched daily. The supply chain is the dominant attack surface for modern cloud platforms, and we treat it accordingly.

§ 05

Data residency and sovereignty

Where data lives matters. HelveX's default and recommended posture is to keep Customer Content in EU-Central regions, which provides a sound legal basis for European customers under GDPR and aligns with our Swiss-headquarters operational footprint. Customers with different requirements can select among multiple supported postures.

Default residency

New tenants are provisioned in the EU-Central region on AWS (eu-central-1, Frankfurt) by default. The control plane, datastores, model inference, and audit logs for the tenant all sit within EU-Central unless the customer explicitly chooses otherwise. EU-Central is governed by GDPR and benefits from the protections of European data-protection law.

Swiss residency

Customers requiring Swiss residency may select GCP's europe-west6 (Zurich) region for storage of Customer Content. Inference for Swiss-resident customers can be served from Zurich-region capacity where available; for the frontier model (Prometheus), some inference traffic may route to EU-Central if Zurich-region capacity is exhausted. The customer is notified, the routing is logged, and the legal basis for the in-EEA transfer is documented.

Your-cloud deployment

For enterprise customers whose risk tolerance requires that Customer Content never leave their own cloud tenancy, HelveX deploys the platform inside the customer's AWS or GCP account. The customer owns the data, the network perimeter, and the encryption keys; HelveX operates the platform as a managed service. In this posture, no Customer Content traverses HelveX-controlled infrastructure.

Cross-border transfers

Customer Content moves across borders only under one of three legal mechanisms: (a) intra-EEA transfer between Member States, which is permitted without additional safeguards under GDPR; (b) transfer to a jurisdiction covered by a European Commission adequacy decision; or (c) transfer subject to the European Commission Standard Contractual Clauses (Decision (EU) 2021/914), supplemented as required by technical and organisational measures including encryption, pseudonymisation, and contractual access restrictions. For Swiss-origin data, our DPA includes the Swiss Addendum aligning the SCCs with Swiss FADP / nFADP requirements.

§ 06

Subprocessors

HelveX engages subprocessors only where doing so is necessary to deliver the Services, and only where the subprocessor's security posture meets or exceeds our internal standard. Our subprocessor governance follows a category-based model: customers know which categories of services we depend on, and the specific provider within each category is disclosed under MNDA. This approach lets us swap providers within a category without renegotiating customer agreements while preserving the customer's ability to evaluate the underlying risk.

Categories of subprocessors

HelveX uses the following categories of subprocessors. Specific provider names within each category are disclosed in Annex C of our Data Processing Addendum, available under MNDA from trust@helvex.ai.

  • Cloud infrastructure — AWS and GCP serve as our primary cloud infrastructure providers, hosting compute, storage, networking, and platform services in the regions described above. Azure is available as an opt-in for enterprise deployments with a strategic preference for that platform.
  • Transactional email delivery — a single provider handles outbound transactional emails (account verification, password reset, security notifications). No marketing email touches Customer Content.
  • Session telemetry — a provider collects pseudonymised performance telemetry from the workspace to support debugging and optimisation. Telemetry payloads exclude prompts, completions, and Customer Content.
  • Error monitoring — a provider receives application error reports. Error payloads are scrubbed of Customer Content before transmission; PII filters operate at the SDK level.
  • Payment processing — a PCI-DSS-compliant provider handles credit-card and ACH transactions on our behalf. HelveX never stores raw card numbers in its own systems.
  • Customer support tooling — a provider hosts our support ticketing system. Customers retain control of which content is shared with support and may use the platform without sharing tickets where their plan permits.

Change management

HelveX provides customers with at least 30 days advance notice of any material change to its subprocessor list (addition of a new subprocessor processing Customer Content, or change in the category of an existing subprocessor). Notice is delivered to the customer's designated administrative contact and published on the Trust Center page. Customers may object to the proposed change; the consequences of objection and our process for resolving objections are described in the Data Processing Addendum.

Commitment

Customer Content is processed solely to provide the Services to the Customer. Customer Content is never used to train HelveX base models. This is a contractual commitment, not a default that customers must opt out of.

§ 07

Access controls

Access to HelveX systems is governed by a single, unified identity and authorisation programme that applies equally to internal staff, contractors, integration partners, and customer administrators acting on their own tenants. The principles below describe how access is granted, used, reviewed, and revoked.

Role-based access control

Within the HelveX platform, customer administrators assign users to roles. Roles encode the set of resources and actions a user may perform within a tenant. We ship a set of standard roles (Owner, Admin, Member, Auditor, Read-Only) sufficient for most organisations and we support fully custom roles for enterprise deployments. Roles can be scoped to specific projects, agents, knowledge bases, or spaces, allowing administrators to enforce least privilege within a tenant.

Single sign-on

The platform supports SAML 2.0 and OpenID Connect for federated authentication. Enterprise tenants can require SSO for all members, disabling local password authentication entirely. Just-in-time provisioning creates user accounts on first SSO login according to attribute mappings the administrator defines; SCIM 2.0 integration is available for tenants who require directory-driven lifecycle management.

Multi-factor authentication

Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for all HelveX staff accessing internal systems. For customer-facing tenants, MFA is enabled by default and can be enforced for all members through tenant-level policy. Supported factors include TOTP authenticator apps, WebAuthn / FIDO2 hardware tokens, and platform authenticators (Touch ID, Face ID, Windows Hello). SMS-based MFA is supported only as a fallback and is not recommended.

Production access

HelveX staff do not have standing access to production systems handling Customer Content. Access is granted through a just-in-time elevation workflow: an engineer requests time-bound access citing a specific operational need (such as an open incident or a customer-authorised support request); the request is reviewed by a peer or manager; access is granted for a bounded window (typically four hours); and the elevation is logged with full context.

Access reviews

Customer administrators have visibility into all users, roles, and assignments within their tenant through the workspace. HelveX conducts internal quarterly access reviews covering all staff and contractor entitlements; reviewers are independent of the access requester and must positively reaffirm each entitlement or the entitlement is automatically revoked. The access-review process is one of the controls evaluated annually under our SOC 2 Type II examination.

§ 08

Logging and audit trails

HelveX operates a comprehensive audit-logging programme that captures every consequential action in the platform. Audit logging is the foundation for incident investigation, regulatory inquiry response, and customer-driven analytics on their own usage of the platform.

What we log

The platform logs, at minimum: every prompt submitted to a model and the corresponding completion; every tool invocation, including the tool identifier, arguments, and result; every data access event against the Knowledge surface, including read, write, and delete operations; every authentication attempt, both successful and failed; every administrative configuration change, including role assignments, key rotations, and tenant-level settings; and every export operation including file downloads and API exports.

Each log record includes actor identity (the user or service principal that initiated the action), tenant identifier, timestamp (in coordinated universal time, to millisecond precision), source IP address, request and response correlation identifiers, and the outcome of the action. Log records are append-only from the perspective of the application tier and are protected by tamper-evident integrity checks.

Retention and access

Default retention for audit logs is 13 months, which exceeds the typical regulatory minimum of 12 months and accommodates customers operating on annual audit cycles. Enterprise customers may extend retention through their order form or shorten it where data-minimisation policies require. Customer administrators have read access to their tenant's audit logs through the workspace and through a Logs API; HelveX staff access customer audit logs only under the standard production-access workflow described in section 7.

Export to customer SIEM

Customers may export audit logs to their own security information and event management (SIEM) system. The platform supports export to industry-standard formats (JSON Lines, CEF) and integration with the native log-routing services of AWS (CloudWatch Logs, S3, Kinesis Firehose) and GCP (Cloud Logging, Pub/Sub, BigQuery). Customers configure routing through the tenant administration surface; HelveX does not retain visibility into the customer's downstream SIEM.

§ 09

Vulnerability management

Software is never finished. HelveX operates a continuous vulnerability-management programme covering the platform, the workspace, the corporate environment, and the third-party software supply chain. The programme is calibrated to identify, classify, prioritise, and remediate vulnerabilities at a tempo that stays ahead of practical exploitation.

Scanning cadence

Software composition analysis runs on every code change and on a continuous schedule against deployed artifacts, identifying known vulnerabilities in third-party dependencies. Static application security testing runs on every pull request before merge. Dynamic application security testing runs nightly against staging environments. Container image scanning runs on every image build and continuously against deployed images. Infrastructure-as-code scanning runs on every change to platform configuration.

Penetration testing

HelveX engages an independent specialist firm to conduct a full-scope penetration test of the platform annually. The scope includes the public web surface, the authenticated workspace, the model inference API, and the underlying control plane. The annual test is supplemented by targeted assessments commissioned for major releases or architectural changes. A summary of the most recent annual penetration test is available to customers under MNDA from trust@helvex.ai.

Responsible disclosure

HelveX operates a coordinated disclosure programme for external security researchers. Reports may be submitted to security@helvex.ai; we respond to substantive reports within five business days, agree on a coordinated disclosure timeline of up to 90 days, and credit researchers in our security advisory when they request acknowledgment. We extend safe-harbour treatment to good-faith research conducted within the scope of our disclosure policy.

§ 10

Incident response

Incident response is the discipline of operating predictably when the platform is not. HelveX's incident-response programme follows a documented playbook that has been refined through quarterly tabletop exercises and through the experience of actual production incidents. We describe the programme below at a level of detail appropriate for customer evaluation; the playbook itself is internal and is reviewed by our SOC 2 auditor.

Detection

Detection sources include automated monitoring of platform health and security telemetry, automated alerts on anomalous authentication and data-access patterns, third-party threat intelligence, internal-tooling reports from engineers, and customer reports. Each detection source feeds a single incident-management system that triages and assigns ownership.

Classification

Incidents are classified by severity (S1 critical, S2 high, S3 medium, S4 low) based on a documented decision matrix that considers the affected systems, the number of tenants impacted, the type and volume of Customer Content potentially involved, and the regulatory implications. Classification is reassessed continuously as the incident evolves.

Customer notification

For incidents that constitute a Personal Data Breach affecting Customer Content (as defined under GDPR Article 4(12)), HelveX commits to notify the affected Customer's designated contacts without undue delay and in any event within 72 hours of confirmation of the breach, consistent with GDPR Article 33. For high-severity incidents (S1) regardless of breach status, our internal policy is to provide first notification within four hours of confirmation. Notifications include the nature of the incident, the categories and approximate number of affected data subjects (where determinable), the likely consequences, and the measures taken or proposed to address the incident and mitigate adverse effects.

Commitment

HelveX will notify Customers of confirmed Personal Data Breaches affecting their Customer Content within 72 hours of confirmation, with intermediate updates as the investigation progresses.

Post-mortem

For S1 and S2 incidents, HelveX produces a written post-mortem document that includes an incident timeline, root-cause analysis, contributing-factor analysis, and a list of remediation actions with named owners and due dates. Post-mortems are reviewed by an internal review board and the resulting action items are tracked to completion. Summaries of S1 and S2 post-mortems are available to affected Customers on request through their account team.

§ 11

Business continuity

HelveX's business-continuity programme is engineered to keep the platform running through the loss of individual components, individual cloud zones, and entire regional failures, and to restore service rapidly even in the unlikely event of a catastrophic loss.

Targets

HelveX commits to the following targets at the platform control-plane level: a Recovery Point Objective (RPO) of one hour or better, meaning that in the event of a recovery the maximum loss of in-flight state is bounded to the most recent one-hour window; and a Recovery Time Objective (RTO) of four hours or better, meaning that the workspace control plane will be restored to operational status within four hours of a declared disaster.

Backup strategy

All Customer Content datastores are backed up continuously via point-in-time recovery (PITR) in the underlying cloud-provider managed services, with backups encrypted using customer-isolated keys and replicated to a geographically distinct region within the same legal jurisdiction. Backup integrity is verified through automated restore tests on a documented cadence. Backup retention defaults to 30 days and can be extended for enterprise tenants whose regulatory regimes require longer windows.

Multi-region failover

The platform control plane operates active-active across multiple availability zones within each region, providing automatic failover at the zone level. For region-level failures, the control plane is deployed in a warm-standby configuration in a secondary region within the same legal jurisdiction (for EU-Central tenants, the secondary is another EU region). Failover to the secondary region is a manual decision that follows a documented runbook; the runbook is exercised in quarterly tabletop drills.

Tabletop and live exercises

HelveX conducts quarterly tabletop exercises covering business-continuity scenarios (regional outage, key-management failure, supply-chain incident, large-scale customer-impacting incident) and annual live-failover exercises in non-production environments. Lessons learned are integrated into runbooks and operational tooling.

§ 12

AI governance

HelveX develops and operates language models. Operating language models at scale requires an AI-governance programme that addresses model behaviour, model deprecation, model abuse, and the obligations of customers who build on top of our models. This section describes how we discharge those responsibilities.

Model cards and evaluations

For each production model (Ether, Nexus, Prometheus), HelveX maintains a model card describing intended use cases, known limitations, training-data sources at the category level, evaluation results on standard public benchmarks, and any deployment-time mitigations. Model cards are versioned alongside the models themselves and published with sufficient detail to support customer due-diligence under the EU AI Act and analogous regimes. We evaluate models against safety, capability, and bias benchmarks before promotion to production, and we re-evaluate at every major model update.

Prompt-injection defences

Prompt injection is the dominant class of practical AI-security failures. HelveX layers defences at multiple points in the inference path: input sanitisation at the API surface; context-isolation between system prompts, user inputs, and tool outputs; output classification to detect and flag suspected injection-driven outputs; and rate limiting and behavioural anomaly detection on agent workloads. Customers building agentic systems on Prometheus are provided with developer-facing guidance and tooling to mitigate prompt injection in their own application layer.

Output classification and abuse monitoring

Outputs are classified at inference time for the presence of harmful content categories (illegal content, content endangering minors, weapons-of-mass-destruction guidance, targeted harassment, intimate imagery without consent, and other categories defined in the Acceptable Use Policy). Classifications are used to enforce safety policies, to surface flags to customer administrators, and as input to abuse-monitoring systems. Abuse-monitoring is conducted at the tenant level on aggregated, pseudonymised signals; HelveX does not read Customer Content for any purpose other than the specific operational needs of providing the Services.

Model deprecation

When a production model is to be deprecated (replaced by a successor or retired entirely), HelveX provides customers with at least 180 days of advance notice, a clear migration path including pricing parity for the recommended successor model, and continued availability of the deprecated model for the notice period. Model deprecations are announced through the Trust Center page and through direct notification to customer administrative contacts. Deprecation is one of the most disruptive events a model-platform customer can experience, and we manage it accordingly.

EU AI Act alignment

HelveX has assessed the production models against the EU AI Act's risk classification framework. None of the production models is, in itself, a high-risk AI system under Annex III of the AI Act. Customers who deploy our models in support of high-risk use cases (such as critical infrastructure operation, education access decisions, employment, essential public services, law enforcement, migration, or administration of justice) bear the obligations of high-risk-system deployers under the Act; HelveX provides the technical documentation and disclosures required to support those obligations, including the model cards described above. For general-purpose AI obligations (Articles 51 to 56), HelveX maintains the required documentation, transparency disclosures, and incident-reporting workflows.

§ 13

Shared responsibility model

Cloud and AI platforms operate under a shared-responsibility model. Security is a joint product of the platform provider's controls and the customer's controls. HelveX cannot do everything; customers cannot do everything; only together do we form a defensible posture. This section makes the boundary explicit.

What HelveX is responsible for

HelveX is responsible for the security of the platform: the physical security of the underlying cloud-provider infrastructure (delegated to AWS, GCP, and their certified facilities); the operational security of the platform control plane, including patching, configuration management, vulnerability remediation, and identity-system hardening; the security of the model inference path, including model-weight provenance, supply-chain integrity, and inference-time abuse monitoring; the encryption of Customer Content at rest and in transit; the maintenance of certifications and audit programmes; and the continuous monitoring and incident response of the platform itself.

What the Customer is responsible for

The Customer is responsible for the security in the platform: the choice of which content is uploaded to the platform; the configuration of access controls, including role assignments, SSO enforcement, MFA enforcement, and audit-review cadence within the tenant; the security of the Customer's own end users' credentials and devices; the policies governing the use of HelveX outputs in the Customer's own products and decision processes; the appropriate disclosures the Customer must make to its own data subjects under applicable law; and the security of any systems the Customer integrates with HelveX through outbound webhooks, API integrations, or agent tools.

Where the boundary moves

For your-cloud deployments, the boundary shifts: the Customer becomes responsible for the operational security of the underlying infrastructure (because the Customer owns it), while HelveX retains responsibility for the platform software running on top. The exact boundary in a your-cloud deployment is documented in the deployment-specific runbook produced during onboarding.

§ 14

Requesting reports

Customers, prospective customers, and regulators may request the following trust-package artifacts under mutual non-disclosure agreement: the current SOC 2 Type II report; the current ISO 27001 certificate and statement of applicability; the most recent independent penetration test summary; the Data Processing Addendum with current Annex C subprocessor list; and the records of processing activities relevant to the Customer's use of the platform.

Requests should be directed to trust@helvex.ai from a corporate domain. The request should identify the requesting organisation, the responsible point of contact, and the artifacts requested. We acknowledge requests within two business days and deliver the requested artifacts, under MNDA, within ten business days of MNDA execution.
