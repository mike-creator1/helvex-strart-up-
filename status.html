What we monitor

The surfaces on the dashboard.

Six surfaces, each with its own probes — health, latency, and error-rate at the user-visible boundary. We target 99.9% availability across the public API, the inference surfaces, and the workspace. The number above each row is the rolling 90-day reading.

Surface 01

Ether API.

The streaming inference surface. Token-time, time-to-first-byte, and end-to-end completion all monitored per region, with a regression alarm at the 95th percentile.

Surface 02

Nexus API.

The long-context reasoning surface and the Citations engine. Probes track retrieval quality, citation coverage, and answer latency at long-prompt sizes.

Surface 03

Prometheus & computer-use.

The agentic runtime, the browser surface, the tool-call gateway. Probes exercise multi-step plans, retries, and approval gates end to end.

Surface 04

Workspace dashboard.

The web app — Chat, Projects, Agents, Knowledge, Spaces, History. Login latency, page-load percentiles, and the realtime stream all probed continuously.

Surface 05

Citations engine.

The retrieval and grounding layer underneath Nexus answers. Probed independently because a degradation here looks fine in the API but breaks trust in the product.

Surface 06

Ingestion pipelines.

The connectors that sync Drive, Notion, Slack, GitHub, and the rest. Probes confirm fresh content lands in the index inside the freshness window for each connector.

Incident process

Three steps. Same every time.

When something breaks, what happens next is on record before it happens. The point is not to make incidents disappear — it is to make them legible to the people running on top of HelveX while we work the fix.

  1. Step 01

    Post here

    The incident appears on this page within minutes of being detected. The first post says what is affected, what the visible symptom looks like, and what we know about the cause. Each update is dated, plain English, and written by the engineer on the bridge.

  2. Step 02

    Email subscribers

    Customers subscribed to status updates get an email when the incident is opened, when the impact changes meaningfully, and when the all-clear lands. Enterprise customers with named support engineers also get a direct call for anything that crosses a severity threshold.

  3. Step 03

    Post-mortem

    A written post-mortem lands inside five working days of the incident resolving. It covers timeline, root cause, customer impact, mitigations, and the specific changes we made to prevent the same shape of incident from recurring. Post-mortems are public.
