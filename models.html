Real-time

Ether

Ether is what the platform reaches for when intelligence has to feel invisible. First token under 100 ms in every region we serve. Streaming-first across the stack. The compute footprint is small, the wait is unnoticeable, and the answers are accurate enough for the work that doesn't have time to wait.

We tuned Ether on conversation, summarisation, drafting, and translation — the work that happens dozens of times a day. It is not designed to outperform Nexus on three-step reasoning or Prometheus on tool use. It is designed to disappear.

Where Ether earns its keep

→
First-line customer support — resolves tickets at conversational latency, hands off when it's out of its depth.
→
Inbox & calendar — drafting, summarising, scheduling, and translating, fast enough that people forget the AI is involved.
→
Live translation — 14 languages with voice, 28 with text. Speaker timing is preserved in voice replies.
→
Quick takes on long context — drop in a 200-page report, get a one-paragraph summary in five seconds.

Reasoning

Nexus

Nexus is the model we hand the questions that deserve thought. It spends compute where it pays off — adaptive depth per token, citations on every claim, and a one-million-token window that holds whole books, full case files, and the kind of multi-source corpora that don't fit in shorter contexts.

Nexus is not the fastest model on the platform. It is the one we trust with answers people will act on. Each cited claim links back to the passage it came from. When Nexus is unsure, it says so — and points at the gap in the source material that left it that way.

Where Nexus earns its keep

→
Investment briefs — reads filings, transcripts, and press releases, returns a position with sourced claims.
→
Scientific review — surveys the literature, surfaces consensus and disagreement, names what hasn't been studied yet.
→
Policy & legal analysis — walks through complex regulation end to end, with the source clauses cited inline.
→
Mathematical and technical work — multi-step proofs, reproducible derivations, and check-it-yourself reasoning chains.

Agentic

Prometheus

Prometheus is the model we put behind tasks that need actually doing. It plans, takes the steps, asks for input only when it has to, and leaves a deterministic trace you can replay step by step. Multi-hour runs, branching sub-agents, approval gates on the irreversible — the engineering reality of "agents that work."

Prometheus is not magic. It is a model trained to read its own plan, grade it, edit it, and notice when something has gone wrong. Most of the engineering effort behind it is invested in making the answer to "how did that happen?" something you can answer quickly, with evidence.

Where Prometheus earns its keep

→
Operational workflows — multi-step jobs across the documents and tools your team already uses, with approval gates on the destructive moves.
→
Long-horizon research agents — full investigations finished overnight, with a replay you can audit from the team's morning standup.
→
Knowledge work that ships — long-form drafting, synthesis, and reporting in a single autonomous pass.
→
Coding & integrations — read repositories, write changes, run tests, and propose patches in a way a human reviewer can defend.

The router

Don't pick one if you don't want to.

HelveX picks a model for every turn based on what the message actually needs. Latency, depth, and cost targets are honoured, and the routing decision is recorded with the response so you can audit it.

01
Latency-bound first. If your target is sub-200ms, the router only considers Ether unless escalation is explicitly requested.
02
Depth-bound second. Long-form analysis with citations is routed to Nexus once the prompt clearly demands research.
03
Action-bound third. When tools are required and the task spans more than a single response, Prometheus takes over.
04
You override anywhere. Pin a model on a chat, a project, or a single message. The router stops guessing.
