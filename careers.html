What we look for

Four traits, in roughly this order.

Resumes get a careful read. They are not how we decide. Every offer we have ever made traces back to the four traits below, in roughly the order they appear.

01 · Senior

You have shipped before.

Not "you have led a team that shipped." You have personally taken a hard problem from an empty repo to production. We hire that pattern more than we hire any specific stack.

02 · Owns end-to-end

You don't wait for a handoff.

You write the spec, you write the eval, you write the runbook. You sit with the customer for the rollout. You write the post-mortem when something breaks. There is no shadow PM behind you.

03 · Writes before code

A doc lands first.

Before you open an editor on a project that will outlive a week, you write the one-page brief — the problem, the constraints, the eval. If you cannot write the brief, you do not yet understand the problem.

04 · Ships every week

Something real, every Friday.

A weekly cadence of small, observable progress beats heroic month-long sprints. The work is on a branch, behind a flag, in a customer's tenant — never in a slide.

How we work

Zurich-based. Senior-paced. Written-first.

A small senior team, an office in Kreis 4, and engineering across three continents. The rhythm below is what every week looks like — for everyone, from the founders down.

Zurich plus global remote. Most roles are based in Zurich on a hybrid pattern; some are full remote across the EU. Forward-deployed engineers travel to where the customer is — London, New York, sometimes further. We pay relocation for anyone moving to Zurich.

A weekly ship cadence. Every Friday is a written check-in: what shipped this week, what is in review, what slipped and why. No standups. No status meetings. The cadence is the planning system.

No on-call burnout. Production incidents do happen — and a rotation exists for them — but the team is large enough that no one carries the pager for more than a week at a time, and a paid recovery day follows a major incident. We do not glamourise heroics.

Written-first culture. Proposals are documents, not slides. Reviews happen in the margin. The senior people on this team got senior by writing — they continue to write, and they expect you to.

Small team, senior compensation. Headcount is not a goal. The path here is to be paid well enough that you do not think about money, and trusted enough that you do not need a manager. The bar to come in is high, and so is the floor.
