House rules

Three rules. That is the whole moderation policy.

  1. 01Argue with evidence. Eval numbers, traces, reproductions — those move discussions forward. Vibes do not.
  2. 02No customer data in public channels. Strip it before you paste, or use the private DM with the team.
  3. 03Ship before you sell. Plug your project after you have shared something useful, not before.

Channels

Four places to find the conversation.

A small set of channels — picked so the same conversation does not happen in four places. Workflow swaps and prompt patterns happen in the forum; bug reports and feature requests live on GitHub.

Channel 01

Discussion forum.

Long-form, threaded, searchable. Workflow write-ups, eval results, prompt patterns. The place we point to when someone says "I solved this last week."

Channel 02

Discord.

Real-time, low-noise. Quick questions, live troubleshooting, the occasional rant about tokenisation. Moderated by the HelveX team during EU and US business hours.

Channel 03

GitHub discussions.

Where issues, feature requests, and the open-source evals live. If your conversation has a code snippet in it, it probably belongs here.

Channel 04

Monthly call.

A standing video call, last Wednesday of the month. The engineer who shipped the most interesting thing in the cycle walks through it, then takes questions for an hour.

Code of conduct

Three lines that hold up.

The house rules above set the tone. The Code of Conduct sets the floor. It is short on purpose — three lines that hold up under almost every situation a community can throw at them.

  • 01 Respect everyone. Disagreement with an argument is welcome. Disagreement with a person is not. Assume the best reading of what someone wrote, especially when the topic is technical and emotions run high.
  • 02 No harassment. Personal attacks, slurs, threats, sexual content, doxxing — banned on first offence, no warning. The moderation team is small and acts fast.
  • 03 No recruiting in channel. The community is a place to swap workflow knowledge, not to sell roles. DM is allowed; broadcast is not. We have careers@ for the other direction.
