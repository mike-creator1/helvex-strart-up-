Model picker

A practical
decision matrix.

Every task has a shape. The wrong model is rarely 'wrong' in the abstract — it is wrong for what the work actually demands. Use the matrix below as a starting point.

If the work is … Pick Why Cost shape
High-volume support, drafting, voice Ether Latency dominates the experience; depth matters less than feeling instant. low · per-token
Investment briefs, regulatory analysis Nexus Long-context reading with cited claims; the answer has to defend itself. medium · per-turn
Operational workflows across tools Prometheus Multi-step planning, tool use, approval gates, and a replayable trace. higher · per-run
Math, proofs, multi-step derivations Nexus Adaptive depth lets it spend the budget where the chain is hardest. medium · per-turn
Live translation, captioning, dictation Ether Streaming-first audio path; speaker timing preserved on voice replies. low · per-second
Code review, repo-wide refactors Prometheus Reads the tree, runs tests, proposes a patch a reviewer can defend. higher · per-run
Single-file edits, focused snippets Ether Short context, fast feedback loop; calls Nexus for tricky reasoning blocks. low · per-token
Overnight research investigations Prometheus Hours-long horizon, branching sub-agents, replay you can audit at standup. higher · per-run

Cost shape describes the dominant unit of pricing for the model in this mode of work. Exact rates appear in the workspace's billing view; volume discounts and committed-use pricing are negotiated for Team and Enterprise.

How routing works

The router picks. You can override.

By default, HelveX picks the cheapest model that meets the quality bar for the task in front of it — and tells you which one it picked, in the same response. If you have a reason to choose differently, pinning takes one click and sticks for the thread, the Project, or the agent.

Default behaviour

Cheapest model that clears the bar.

The router reads the prompt, the attached tools, the context window, and the latency budget. It picks the cheapest of Ether, Nexus, or Prometheus that we believe will land the task — and it shows the choice in the response header so you can sanity-check it.

Pinning

One click. Sticks.

Pin Ether on a Chat thread when you need it to feel instant. Pin Nexus on a Project when every reply needs citations. Pin Prometheus on an agent when there are tools to call and a horizon to plan over. Pinning sticks until you change it.

Latency-bound

Pin Ether.

Streaming-first work, voice, drafting, anything where the experience is the latency. Ether is the streaming surface and the lowest cost-per-token of the three. If a response under one second is the requirement, the router will still pick it — but pinning removes any ambiguity.

Depth-bound

Pin Nexus.

Long-context reading, citation work, math, regulatory analysis, the kind of brief where the answer has to defend itself. Nexus has the longest context window and the Citations engine. Pin it on anything that goes in front of a board or a regulator.

Action-bound

Pin Prometheus.

Tools to call, computer use, web search, planning over hours, a chained set of approvals. Prometheus is the agentic surface and the only model with the multi-tool plan critique loop. Pin it on anything that has to actually go and do the thing.

When to override

Three scenarios. Override without thinking.

The router is good — not psychic. There are three patterns where the right answer is to pin, and to pin in the same direction every time. If you find yourself in one of these, do not let the router pick.

  1. Scenario 01

    PII-sensitive

    If the run touches personally identifiable information — names, account numbers, health data, anything you would not want leaving your tenant — pin your-cloud Nexus. The model serves from your AWS or GCP region with zero-retention mode available, and the citations engine keeps every claim traceable.

  2. Scenario 02

    Long-running

    If the agent has to plan over more than a few minutes, call multiple tools in sequence, or branch and retry, pin Prometheus. Long-horizon work is what Prometheus is for, and switching mid-run loses the plan-critique state that makes the replay useful.

  3. Scenario 03

    Throughput batch

    If you are running thousands of similar items through the same prompt — enrichment passes, classification, ingestion clean-up — pin Ether. The cost-per-token is the lowest of the three and the streaming surface holds up under load. Save Nexus and Prometheus for the items that fail the Ether pass.
