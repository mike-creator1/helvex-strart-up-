What we ship most often

Four engagement shapes.

Beneath the individual stories sits a pattern. Most of the work we deliver falls into one of four shapes — the same kind of engagement, with a senior engineer owning it from brief to operate-mode.

Shape 01

A production support agent in six weeks.

A senior engineer takes the support inbox, the runbook, and the historical tickets and ships a first-line agent into the customer's tenant. Approval gates on outbound, hand-off on anything unfamiliar, replay trace on every reply.

Shape 02

Enterprise RAG over a company wiki.

Drive, Notion, SharePoint, and the ticket archive indexed with permissions intact. A Nexus-powered surface that returns the passage and links to the source on every claim. Citations are the answer; the paraphrase is the helper.

Shape 03

An internal sales-ops assistant.

An agent that lives in the CRM, fills new leads with firmographics and recent news, drafts the first reply, and writes the account brief before the rep opens the record. Connected to the calendar, the call notes, and the playbook.

Shape 04

A senior-engineer-led integration sprint.

A six-to-ten-week cycle where the engineer is on-site or embedded with the customer's team. Output is a working workflow in their tenant plus the runbook to keep it useful. Operate-mode retainer picks up from there.
