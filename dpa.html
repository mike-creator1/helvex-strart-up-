Data Processing Addendum

This Data Processing Addendum (the "DPA") forms part of the agreement between HelveX AG ("HelveX") and the Customer for the use of the HelveX AI builder, the Hosted Services, the APIs, and the surrounding services (the "Services"), and implements Article 28 GDPR and substantially equivalent provisions of the Swiss FADP / nFADP and the UK GDPR. The DPA governs HelveX's processing of Personal Data on behalf of the Customer in connection with the Services.

EffectiveMay 2026 Last updatedMay 2026 Version1.0 Governing lawSwitzerland
§ 01

Preamble and application

This DPA forms an integral part of the Terms of Service published at /terms and any Order Form executed between HelveX and the Customer. By accepting the Terms of Service or by executing an Order Form, the Customer agrees to this DPA. Where the Customer has executed a separate written data-processing agreement with HelveX, that separate agreement prevails to the extent of any conflict.

This DPA applies whenever HelveX processes Personal Data on behalf of the Customer in connection with the Services. In particular, the DPA governs HelveX's processing of (i) Personal Data of End Users that flows through Generated Applications the Customer builds and deploys on HelveX, and (ii) Personal Data the Customer provides to HelveX as a Builder Input, regardless of whether that Personal Data concerns the Customer's own personnel, the Customer's customers, or any third party. The DPA implements Article 28 GDPR and equivalent provisions of the Swiss FADP / nFADP and the UK GDPR.

The DPA does not govern HelveX's processing of Customer account data, billing data, support correspondence, and telemetry generated by the Customer's use of the Builder for HelveX's own purposes (security, fraud prevention, abuse detection, billing reconciliation, service operation, and aggregated product improvement). For such processing HelveX is an independent Controller and the Privacy Policy governs.

§ 02

Definitions

For the purpose of this DPA, the following terms have the meanings given below. Capitalised terms not defined here have the meaning given in the Terms of Service.

"Applicable Data Protection Law"
All laws and regulations applicable to the processing of Personal Data under this DPA, including the GDPR, the Swiss FADP / nFADP, the UK GDPR, the CCPA / CPRA, and equivalent regimes.
"Builder Personal Data"
Personal Data that the Customer provides to HelveX or that is generated about the Customer's own use of the Builder, including Personal Data embedded in Builder Inputs, AI Output, project metadata, and project state, but excluding the Customer account data, billing data, support correspondence, and HelveX-controlled telemetry described in §1.
"Controller"
The natural or legal person which alone or jointly with others determines the purposes and means of the processing of Personal Data, as defined by the GDPR.
"Data Subject"
An identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates.
"Generated Application Personal Data"
Personal Data of End Users that is collected by or flows through a Generated Application that the Customer builds and operates using the Services.
"Personal Data"
Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person processed by HelveX on the Customer's behalf in connection with the Services. Where Applicable Data Protection Law uses a different defined term (for example, "Personal Information" under the CCPA / CPRA), that term is treated as substantially equivalent for the purposes of this DPA.
"Personal Data Breach"
A breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise processed by HelveX in connection with the Services.
"Processor"
A natural or legal person which processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller, as defined by the GDPR.
"Processing"
Any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, as defined by the GDPR.
"Restricted Transfer"
A transfer of Personal Data subject to a cross-border transfer requirement under Applicable Data Protection Law, including transfers from the EEA, Switzerland, or the United Kingdom to a third country not benefiting from an adequacy decision.
"SCCs"
The Standard Contractual Clauses approved by the European Commission in Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914 of 4 June 2021, as may be amended or replaced.
"Sub-processor"
A third party engaged by HelveX to process Personal Data on HelveX's behalf in support of the Services.
"Swiss Addendum"
The addendum published by the Swiss FDPIC for the application of the SCCs to transfers from Switzerland.
"UK IDTA"
The International Data Transfer Addendum to the EU Commission Standard Contractual Clauses published by the UK Information Commissioner's Office, as may be amended or replaced.
§ 03

Roles and responsibilities

The Parties acknowledge and agree the following allocation of roles.

With respect to Generated Application Personal Data, the Customer is the Controller and HelveX is the Processor. The Customer determines the purposes and means of the processing performed by the Customer's Generated Application; HelveX provides the underlying hosting, storage, networking, and platform services on the Customer's documented instructions, and processes Generated Application Personal Data only as required to provide those services.

With respect to Builder Personal Data, the Customer is the Controller and HelveX is the Processor. The Customer determines the purposes and means of submitting Personal Data to the Builder as a Builder Input or of generating Personal Data through use of the Builder; HelveX processes such Personal Data only as required to provide the Builder Services.

With respect to Customer account data, billing data, support correspondence, and HelveX-controlled telemetry, HelveX is an independent Controller and the Privacy Policy governs. The Customer represents that it has the necessary lawful basis to disclose to HelveX such Personal Data of its own personnel as is required to administer the contract (for example, the names and email addresses of authorised users, billing contacts, and security contacts) and has provided those persons with any required disclosures.

The Customer's documented instructions to HelveX for HelveX's processing as a Processor are the Terms of Service, this DPA, the Order Form, the configuration the Customer applies in the Builder (including chosen processing region, chosen retention configuration, chosen sub-processor scope under enterprise terms), and any further written instructions the Customer issues from time to time. HelveX will inform the Customer if, in HelveX's opinion, an instruction infringes Applicable Data Protection Law.

§ 05

Processor obligations

HelveX undertakes that, as Processor, HelveX will:

  • process Personal Data only on documented instructions from the Customer, including with regard to Restricted Transfers, unless required to process Personal Data by a law to which HelveX is subject (in which case HelveX will inform the Customer of that legal requirement before processing, unless the law prohibits such information on important grounds of public interest);
  • ensure that persons authorised to process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality;
  • implement the technical and organisational measures required by Article 32 GDPR, described at a high level at /security-page and in Annex II;
  • respect the conditions for engaging Sub-processors set out in §6 and §7;
  • taking into account the nature of the processing, assist the Customer by appropriate technical and organisational measures, insofar as this is possible, for the fulfilment of the Customer's obligation to respond to requests for exercising the Data Subject's rights under Chapter III GDPR;
  • assist the Customer in ensuring compliance with the obligations pursuant to Articles 32 to 36 GDPR taking into account the nature of processing and the information available to HelveX;
  • at the choice of the Customer, delete or return all the Personal Data to the Customer after the end of the provision of services relating to processing, and delete existing copies unless Applicable Data Protection Law requires storage of the Personal Data;
  • make available to the Customer all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with the obligations laid down in Article 28 GDPR and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Customer or another auditor mandated by the Customer, subject to §14.
§ 06

Sub-processors

The Customer grants HelveX general prior authorisation to engage Sub-processors in the categories listed in Annex III, subject to the conditions set out in this §6. The current list of specific Sub-processor identities is made available to authorised Customer representatives under MNDA via trust@helvex.ai; the list is also reflected on the Trust Center page to the extent disclosure-able publicly.

Advance notice. HelveX gives the Customer at least thirty (30) days' advance notice before adding a new Sub-processor in a material category or replacing one of HelveX's primary cloud providers, primary foundation-model providers, or primary payment processor. The notice is delivered to the account-billing contact by email and is also posted on the Trust Center. For non-material changes (replacement of a regional support tooling vendor, addition of an additional region of an existing Sub-processor in an already-authorised category), HelveX may give shorter notice consistent with operational needs.

Right to object. The Customer may, on reasonable grounds related to data protection, object to a proposed Sub-processor within fifteen (15) days of HelveX's notice. The Parties will work in good faith to find an alternative arrangement (for example, processing in a different region, use of a different Sub-processor, or scoping changes that avoid use of the objected-to Sub-processor for the Customer's data). If the Parties are unable to find an acceptable arrangement within thirty (30) days of the objection, the Customer may terminate the affected portion of the Services for cause and receive a pro rata refund of any prepaid fees attributable to the unused portion.

Flow-down obligations. HelveX engages each Sub-processor under a written contract that imposes data-protection obligations no less protective than those HelveX owes to the Customer under this DPA, including obligations covering confidentiality, security, breach notification, sub-processor management, international transfers, and audit. HelveX remains fully responsible to the Customer for the performance of each Sub-processor's obligations.

§ 07

AI model providers as Sub-processors

The Services rely in part on third-party foundation model providers ("Model Providers") to produce AI Output in response to Builder Inputs. Where a Builder Input contains Personal Data and is routed through the Services to a Model Provider for the purpose of generating AI Output, the Model Provider acts as a Sub-processor with respect to that Personal Data.

HelveX selects Model Providers that meet HelveX's data-protection due-diligence criteria, which include the following minimum requirements. The Model Provider must (i) contractually commit not to use Customer prompts or the corresponding AI Output to train any model, by default; (ii) commit to retention of prompt and output content at the inference layer for no longer than is needed to deliver and log the request (with operational logs subject to short and defined retention); (iii) provide security commitments substantially equivalent to those HelveX owes the Customer under this DPA; (iv) flow back breach-notification obligations on a timeline consistent with HelveX's onward obligations to the Customer; and (v) accept appropriate cross-border transfer terms (the SCCs as a sub-processor or substantially equivalent terms).

The list of currently engaged Model Providers, the regions in which each operates, and the categorical use case for which each is engaged, is made available to authorised Customer representatives under MNDA via trust@helvex.ai. On enterprise terms, Customers may enable an approved-providers-only mode that restricts routing of the Customer's prompts to a Customer-nominated subset of approved Model Providers; Customer prompts that would otherwise be routed outside that subset are rejected.

HelveX contractually requires foundation-model Sub-processors to commit to no-training on Customer prompts and AI Output. Where a Model Provider materially changes its terms to permit such training, HelveX will discontinue use of that Model Provider for Customer prompts unless and until the Customer opts in to the change in writing.

§ 08

Confidentiality of personnel

HelveX ensures that all personnel with access to Personal Data are bound by written confidentiality obligations as a condition of their employment or engagement, and that those obligations survive the end of the employment or engagement. HelveX restricts access to Personal Data to those personnel who need such access to perform their function under this DPA, in line with the least-privilege controls described in /security-page.

HelveX provides data-protection training to all personnel on onboarding and refreshes the training annually. Personnel in roles with privileged access to Personal Data receive role-specific training on the additional obligations applicable to their role, including secure-handling, incident-reporting, and forensic-preservation duties.

§ 09

Data Subject rights — assistance

HelveX, taking into account the nature of the processing, assists the Customer by appropriate technical and organisational measures, insofar as is possible, for the fulfilment of the Customer's obligation to respond to requests for exercising the Data Subject's rights under Articles 12 to 22 GDPR and equivalent provisions of other Applicable Data Protection Law.

The Builder includes tooling within the Customer's workspace that the Customer can use, on its own initiative, to locate, export, rectify, and delete Personal Data within the Customer's projects and within Generated Applications the Customer has built. The tooling includes search across project data, export in standard formats, in-place edit functions, and deletion functions with audit logging. The Customer is the primary actor with respect to Data Subject requests addressed to data held within the Customer's Generated Applications.

Where a Data Subject submits a request directly to HelveX in connection with the Customer's data, HelveX promptly refers the request to the Customer and does not respond substantively except as the Customer instructs or as Applicable Data Protection Law requires HelveX to respond. HelveX may charge for assistance that goes materially beyond standard support; routine assistance is included within the Services.

§ 10

Personal Data Breach

HelveX notifies the Customer of a Personal Data Breach affecting the Customer's Personal Data without undue delay and in any event within seventy-two (72) hours of HelveX's confirmation of the breach.

The notification is delivered to the account-billing contact and to any additional security contacts the Customer has nominated, by email. The notification describes, in line with Article 33(3) GDPR: (i) the nature of the Personal Data Breach, including, where possible, the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects concerned and the categories and approximate number of personal-data records concerned; (ii) the name and contact details of the data protection officer or other contact point where more information can be obtained; (iii) the likely consequences of the Personal Data Breach; and (iv) the measures taken or proposed to be taken by HelveX to address the Personal Data Breach, including, where appropriate, measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects.

Where it is not possible to provide all of the above information at the same time as the initial notification, HelveX provides the information in phases without further undue delay. HelveX cooperates with the Customer and provides reasonable assistance to the Customer in the Customer's onward obligation to notify supervisory authorities and (where applicable) Data Subjects in accordance with Articles 33 and 34 GDPR. The Customer remains responsible for any onward notification to supervisory authorities and to its own Data Subjects.

HelveX maintains and provides on request a documented record of each Personal Data Breach, including the facts, its effects, and the remedial action taken, sufficient to demonstrate compliance with Article 33(5) GDPR.

§ 11

DPIA and prior consultation assistance

HelveX provides the Customer with reasonable assistance, taking into account the nature of the processing and the information available to HelveX, with the Customer's obligation to carry out Data Protection Impact Assessments under Article 35 GDPR and to undertake prior consultations with supervisory authorities under Article 36 GDPR where the Customer's processing requires them.

Assistance typically takes the form of: documentation describing the nature, scope, and purpose of HelveX's processing as Processor; the technical and organisational measures HelveX implements; the categories of Sub-processor engaged; the transfer-impact assessments HelveX has produced; and responses to reasonable written questions from the Customer's data protection officer or equivalent function.

§ 12

International transfers

The Parties acknowledge that the operation of the Services may require Restricted Transfers of Personal Data. To address such transfers, the Parties agree the following.

SCCs incorporated. The SCCs are incorporated into this DPA by reference and form part of it. The applicable Modules are Module 2 (Controller to Processor) where HelveX is the data importer of Personal Data for which the Customer is the Controller, and Module 3 (Processor to Sub-processor) where HelveX engages a Sub-processor that is a data importer of Personal Data for which the Customer is the Controller. The Parties' choices in the SCCs are as set out in Annex IV.

Swiss Addendum. Where the SCCs apply to a Restricted Transfer of Personal Data originating in Switzerland, the Swiss Addendum published by the Swiss FDPIC is incorporated by reference and forms part of this DPA. References in the SCCs to Member State law are read as references to Swiss law where required by the Swiss Addendum; the supervisory authority is the Swiss FDPIC; and Swiss law governs the SCCs where the data subjects are habitually resident in Switzerland.

UK IDTA. Where the SCCs apply to a Restricted Transfer of Personal Data originating in the United Kingdom, the UK International Data Transfer Addendum is incorporated by reference and forms part of this DPA, configured in accordance with Annex IV.

Adequacy decisions. Where the recipient of a Restricted Transfer is located in a country that benefits from an adequacy decision under Applicable Data Protection Law (for example, where the EU Commission has decided that the country ensures an adequate level of protection), the transfer relies on the adequacy decision and the SCCs apply on a subsidiary basis to the extent the recipient is not within the scope of the adequacy decision.

Supplementary measures. HelveX implements supplementary technical and organisational measures over the contractual transfer mechanism, including: encryption in transit and at rest as described in /security-page and Annex II; access controls implementing least-privilege and multi-factor authentication for personnel; logging of administrative access to support after-the-fact review; data minimisation in cross-border flows; documented Transfer Impact Assessments by category of recipient, available to authorised Customer representatives under MNDA via trust@helvex.ai; and, on enterprise terms, optional regional pinning that confines processing of the Customer's data to a Customer-nominated region.

§ 13

Security measures (technical and organisational)

HelveX implements and maintains the technical and organisational measures required by Article 32 GDPR to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, taking into account the state of the art, the costs of implementation, the nature, scope, context, and purposes of the processing, and the risks for the rights and freedoms of natural persons. The current measures are described at a high level at /security-page and are summarised by category in Annex II. HelveX may update the measures from time to time without diminishing the overall level of protection.

§ 14

Audit rights

The Customer may audit HelveX's compliance with this DPA in accordance with this §14.

Frequency. The Customer may exercise an audit right once per annual period, save where additional audits are required by Applicable Data Protection Law, by a supervisory authority, or following a Personal Data Breach materially affecting the Customer's Personal Data.

Auditor. The audit may be conducted by the Customer or by a mutually-agreed independent third-party auditor bound by written confidentiality obligations no less protective than those between the Parties. HelveX may reasonably object to a proposed third-party auditor that is a HelveX competitor.

Scope and conduct. The scope of the audit is limited to the matters relevant to HelveX's compliance with this DPA. The Customer provides reasonable advance written notice (no less than thirty (30) days, save in emergency), the audit is conducted during HelveX's normal business hours, and the audit must not disrupt HelveX's operations or the use of the Services by other Customers. The Customer's auditors comply with HelveX's reasonable site-security, confidentiality, and data-handling procedures.

Satisfaction by documentary review. HelveX may satisfy the audit obligation in the first instance by providing then-current independent attestation reports against the frameworks named in /security-page where such reports exist; where they do not yet exist, by providing the then-current control-mapping documents, gap-assessment outputs, and the planned attestation timeline; penetration-test summaries; the latest business-continuity test report; and written responses to the Customer's reasonable written questions. Such documentary materials are made available to authorised Customer representatives under MNDA via trust@helvex.ai. Where the Customer can reasonably demonstrate that documentary review is insufficient for the Customer's compliance obligations, an on-site or remote interactive audit will be conducted in accordance with this §14.

Cost allocation. Each Party bears its own costs of audit. The Customer reimburses HelveX's reasonable time for assistance that materially exceeds the standard cooperation thresholds set out in the Order Form, at HelveX's then-current professional-services rates.

§ 15

Return and deletion of Personal Data

Within thirty (30) days of termination or expiry of the Services, HelveX, at the Customer's election made in writing within that period, returns the Personal Data to the Customer or deletes it from primary storage. The Customer's standard export tooling within the Builder is available to assist with return. Where the Customer does not communicate an election within the period, HelveX deletes the Personal Data.

HelveX deletes existing copies of the Personal Data save for copies that HelveX is required to retain by Applicable Data Protection Law or by other applicable law, including the Swiss accounting and tax retention obligations referred to in the Privacy Policy. Such retained copies are subject to the same confidentiality and security obligations as the original Personal Data and are deleted at the end of the statutory retention period.

Backups containing Personal Data are not separately deleted at the moment of termination; instead, they are purged according to the rolling backup-retention schedule described in §11 of the Privacy Policy (rotation cycle not exceeding ninety (90) days). During the backup-retention window, backups remain encrypted, access-controlled, and used only for disaster-recovery purposes.

HelveX provides a written confirmation of deletion on request.

§ 16

Special categories of Personal Data

The Customer warrants that it will not provide to HelveX as a Builder Input, and will not configure a Generated Application to collect, store, or process, any Personal Data falling within the special categories listed in Article 9 GDPR (health data, biometric data processed for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person, genetic data, data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade-union membership, and data concerning a natural person's sex life or sexual orientation) or any criminal-conviction data under Article 10 GDPR, without prior written agreement with HelveX. The prior written agreement must specify the lawful basis on which the Customer relies, the additional technical and organisational safeguards that will apply, and any additional contractual terms.

If, despite the warranty above, the Customer's Generated Application processes special-category Personal Data, the Customer remains the Controller of such data, the Customer remains responsible for identifying and documenting the lawful basis for the processing, and HelveX's processing as Processor remains governed by the standard terms of this DPA.

§ 17

Children's Personal Data

The Customer is responsible for compliance with the rules applicable to Personal Data of children under Article 8 GDPR, COPPA, the UK Age Appropriate Design Code, and substantially equivalent regimes, with respect to any End User Personal Data of children processed through the Customer's Generated Applications. The Customer is responsible for obtaining verifiable parental consent where required, for designing the Customer's Generated Application appropriately for the age group concerned, and for honouring any heightened obligations imposed by Applicable Data Protection Law on the processing of children's Personal Data.

HelveX provides documentation and reasonable processor-side assistance to support such compliance but does not assume the Controller role with respect to End User children's Personal Data and does not, by providing such assistance, accept any of the Customer's controller-side obligations.

§ 18

Liability

Each Party's liability arising out of or related to this DPA is subject to the limitation of liability set out in the Terms of Service, except as expressly provided in this DPA or as required by Applicable Data Protection Law. Nothing in this DPA limits Data Subjects' rights under Article 82 GDPR or excludes either Party's liability that cannot be excluded or limited under Applicable Data Protection Law.

§ 19

Order of precedence, term, governing law

Order of precedence. In the event of any conflict between this DPA, the SCCs, the Terms of Service, the Order Form, and the Privacy Policy, the following order of precedence applies: (i) the SCCs (where they apply to a Restricted Transfer); (ii) this DPA; (iii) the Terms of Service; (iv) the Order Form (except to the extent the Order Form expressly states that it overrides a specific provision of the Terms of Service or this DPA, in which case that express override prevails for the scope of the override only).

Term. This DPA is effective from the effective date of the Customer's agreement with HelveX and continues for the duration of the provision of the Services to the Customer, save for those provisions that by their nature survive termination (including obligations relating to confidentiality, return and deletion of Personal Data, post-termination breach notification with respect to retained back-ups, audit assistance with respect to processing carried out during the term, and liability for processing carried out during the term).

Governing law. Swiss law governs this DPA, harmonised with the GDPR for Personal Data originating in the EEA, harmonised with the Swiss FADP / nFADP for Personal Data originating in Switzerland, and harmonised with the UK GDPR for Personal Data originating in the United Kingdom. The provisions of the SCCs governing the law of the SCCs apply to the SCCs themselves where incorporated. Disputes arising out of or related to this DPA are subject to the dispute-resolution provisions of the Terms of Service, save where Applicable Data Protection Law confers competence on a specific forum (for example, the courts of the Data Subject's habitual residence under Clause 18 of the SCCs).

Clause 7 — Docking Not used as standard; available where the Parties expressly agree in writing to add a further data exporter or importer.
