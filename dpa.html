Data Processing Addendum

This Data Processing Addendum ("DPA") forms part of the Terms of Service between HelveX AG ("Processor") and Customer ("Controller") and applies whenever HelveX processes Personal Data on behalf of Customer in the course of providing the Services. It implements Article 28 of the GDPR and equivalent provisions of the Swiss FADP / nFADP.

EffectiveApril 2026 Last updatedApril 2026 Version1.0 Governing lawSwitzerland
§ 01

Definitions

Unless otherwise defined in this DPA, capitalised terms have the meanings given to them in the Terms of Service. The following GDPR-aligned terms have the meanings set out below; where the Swiss FADP / nFADP applies, equivalent Swiss-law definitions apply mutatis mutandis.

Adequacy Decision
A decision adopted by the European Commission under Article 45 GDPR (or, in the case of Swiss FADP, by the Federal Council) determining that a third country, a territory, or a specified sector ensures an adequate level of protection of Personal Data.
Applicable Data Protection Law
The GDPR (Regulation (EU) 2016/679), the UK GDPR and the UK Data Protection Act 2018, the Swiss FADP and nFADP, the California CCPA / CPRA, and any other data-protection law of general application to the Processing of Personal Data under the Terms.
Controller
The natural or legal person which, alone or jointly with others, determines the purposes and means of the Processing of Personal Data. In this DPA, the Controller is the Customer.
Data Subject
An identified or identifiable natural person to whom Personal Data relates.
Personal Data
Any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person that forms part of the Customer Content Processed under the Terms.
Personal Data Breach
A breach of security leading to the accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure of, or access to, Personal Data transmitted, stored, or otherwise Processed.
Processing
Any operation or set of operations performed on Personal Data, whether or not by automated means, including collection, recording, organisation, structuring, storage, adaptation, retrieval, consultation, use, disclosure, transmission, dissemination, restriction, erasure, or destruction.
Processor
A natural or legal person which Processes Personal Data on behalf of the Controller. In this DPA, the Processor is HelveX AG.
Special Categories of Personal Data
Personal Data revealing racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, trade-union membership; genetic data; biometric data Processed for the purpose of uniquely identifying a natural person; data concerning health; data concerning a natural person's sex life or sexual orientation; or comparable categories under other Applicable Data Protection Law.
Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)
The standard contractual clauses for the transfer of Personal Data to third countries pursuant to Regulation (EU) 2016/679, adopted by the European Commission as Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914 of 4 June 2021.
Sub-processor
A natural or legal person engaged by HelveX (or its affiliate) to Process Personal Data on behalf of Customer in connection with the Services.
Supervisory Authority
An independent public authority established by a Member State under Article 51 GDPR, the Swiss Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner (FDPIC), or any equivalent authority under Applicable Data Protection Law.
§ 02

Subject matter, duration, nature, and purpose of Processing

The subject matter, duration, nature, and purpose of the Processing, together with the types of Personal Data and categories of Data Subjects, are specified in Annex I (Processing Details) to this DPA. The Processing relates to the provision of the Services as defined in the Terms; HelveX Processes Personal Data only to the extent and for the time necessary to provide those Services in accordance with the Controller's documented instructions.

The Controller's documented instructions for the Processing of Personal Data are set out in this DPA, in the Terms, in any Order Form, and in any additional written instructions issued by the Controller from time to time consistent with the Services. The Controller agrees that the configuration of its tenant (including retention settings, access controls, and integration choices) constitutes documented instructions for the corresponding Processing.

§ 03

Roles and responsibilities

The parties agree that, with respect to Personal Data Processed in the course of providing the Services, the Customer is the Controller and HelveX is the Processor. Where the Customer is itself a Processor on behalf of a third-party Controller, HelveX acts as a Sub-processor and references in this DPA to the Customer's instructions include those instructions that the Customer is required to receive from and pass on by the relevant Controller.

Ancillary Processing

HelveX additionally Processes a limited set of Personal Data as an Independent Controller for purposes that arise from the operation of the Services rather than from the Customer's instructions. These ancillary purposes include: (a) account administration (managing Users' authentication credentials, billing contacts); (b) security operations (detection and prevention of fraud, abuse, and unauthorised access); (c) billing and accounting (in accordance with Swiss commercial-code retention requirements); (d) legal compliance (responses to lawful requests from competent authorities); and (e) limited aggregated analytics on platform usage for service improvement. HelveX's role and responsibilities as an Independent Controller for these purposes are governed by HelveX's Privacy Policy and not by this DPA.

Allocation of compliance roles

The Controller is responsible for determining the purposes and means of the Processing and for the lawfulness of the underlying Processing (including the existence of a lawful basis under Article 6 GDPR, compliance with notice obligations to Data Subjects, and compliance with Article 9 GDPR where Special Categories are involved). HelveX, as Processor, is responsible for Processing Personal Data only on the Controller's documented instructions and for implementing the technical and organisational measures set out in Annex II.

§ 04

Processor obligations

HelveX, in its capacity as Processor, undertakes to comply with each of the following obligations.

Documented instructions

HelveX will Process Personal Data only on the documented instructions of the Controller, including with regard to transfers of Personal Data to a third country or an international organisation, except where required to do so by applicable law to which HelveX is subject. In that event, HelveX will inform the Controller of the legal requirement before Processing, unless that law prohibits such information on important grounds of public interest.

No purpose beyond Service delivery

HelveX will not Process Personal Data for any purpose other than providing the Services in accordance with the Terms and this DPA. In particular, HelveX will not use Customer Content (including Personal Data within Customer Content) to train, fine-tune, or otherwise improve the base capabilities of HelveX's models.

Commitment

HelveX does not use Customer Content (including any Personal Data within Customer Content) to train HelveX base models. This is a binding contractual commitment under this DPA, operationalised through technical controls that prevent Customer Content from being routed to training pipelines.

Personnel confidentiality

HelveX will ensure that persons authorised to Process Personal Data have committed themselves to confidentiality or are under an appropriate statutory obligation of confidentiality.

Security of Processing

HelveX will implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, taking into account the state of the art, the costs of implementation, and the nature, scope, context, and purposes of Processing as well as the risk of varying likelihood and severity to the rights and freedoms of natural persons. The specific measures are set out in Annex II (Technical and Organisational Measures).

Assistance with Data Subject rights

HelveX will, taking into account the nature of the Processing, assist the Controller by appropriate technical and organisational measures, insofar as this is possible, for the fulfilment of the Controller's obligation to respond to requests for exercising Data Subjects' rights as set out in Articles 12 to 23 GDPR. The assistance is described in Section 7 of this DPA.

Assistance with compliance obligations

HelveX will assist the Controller in ensuring compliance with the Controller's obligations under Articles 32 to 36 GDPR (security of Processing, notification of Personal Data Breach, communication of Personal Data Breach to Data Subjects, data protection impact assessments, and prior consultation), taking into account the nature of Processing and the information available to HelveX.

Information for Controller compliance demonstration

HelveX will make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with the obligations laid down in Article 28 GDPR and allow for and contribute to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller, as further set out in Section 11 of this DPA.

§ 05

Sub-processors

The Controller hereby authorises HelveX to engage Sub-processors for the Processing of Personal Data on behalf of the Controller, subject to the conditions in this Section 5. This authorisation is a general written authorisation within the meaning of Article 28(2) GDPR.

Current Sub-processors

The current list of authorised Sub-processors is set out in Annex III (Authorised Sub-processors). The list identifies Sub-processors by category, region, and purpose; the specific entity name within each category is available to the Controller under mutual non-disclosure agreement from trust@helvex.ai.

Notice of changes

HelveX will provide the Controller with at least 30 days' advance notice of any intended change in the Sub-processor list affecting Processing of the Controller's Personal Data, including the addition of a new Sub-processor or a change in the category of an existing Sub-processor. Notice will be delivered through the Controller's designated administrative contact and through the Trust Center.

Right to object

The Controller may object to the proposed change on reasonable grounds related to the protection of Personal Data, by written notice to HelveX within the 30-day notice period. On receipt of an objection, HelveX will use commercially reasonable efforts to make available a reasonable alternative that addresses the Controller's concerns. If no such alternative is available within sixty (60) days of the objection, the Controller may, as its sole and exclusive remedy, terminate the affected portion of the Services for cause with a prorated refund of any prepaid, unused fees attributable to the affected portion.

Sub-processor obligations

HelveX will impose on each Sub-processor, by written contract, data-protection obligations that provide a level of protection for Personal Data equivalent to those imposed on HelveX under this DPA, in particular providing sufficient guarantees to implement appropriate technical and organisational measures.

Liability for Sub-processors

Where a Sub-processor fails to fulfil its data-protection obligations, HelveX remains fully liable to the Controller for the performance of the Sub-processor's obligations.

§ 06

Confidentiality of personnel

HelveX ensures that persons authorised to Process Personal Data are bound by appropriate confidentiality undertakings. All HelveX staff and contractors with access to Personal Data are bound by written confidentiality agreements that survive termination of their engagement with HelveX. Confidentiality undertakings cover both the existence and the content of Customer Personal Data, and are reinforced by mandatory training on data-protection responsibilities at onboarding and annually thereafter.

§ 07

Data Subject rights

HelveX, taking into account the nature of the Processing, assists the Controller by appropriate technical and organisational measures, insofar as this is possible, in responding to requests from Data Subjects exercising their rights under Applicable Data Protection Law, including the rights of access, rectification, erasure, restriction of Processing, data portability, objection, and the right not to be subject to automated decision-making.

Assistance modalities

The principal means by which HelveX assists the Controller are: (a) self-service capabilities within the workspace and through the API by which the Controller can retrieve, export, rectify, restrict, or delete Personal Data on a Data-Subject-specific basis; (b) tenant-level data export tools enabling structured retrieval of Personal Data in machine-readable format; (c) audit logging that enables the Controller to verify and demonstrate fulfilment of Data Subject requests; and (d) where the foregoing self-service capabilities are insufficient, manual assistance from HelveX upon Controller's reasonable request, at no additional charge for assistance directly responsive to Data Subject rights.

Direct requests from Data Subjects

If HelveX receives a request directly from a Data Subject whose Personal Data HelveX Processes on behalf of the Controller, HelveX will (a) not respond to the request itself except to acknowledge receipt and direct the Data Subject to the Controller, unless required to do so by applicable law; (b) promptly notify the Controller of the request, including any deadline by which the Controller must respond under Applicable Data Protection Law; and (c) cooperate with the Controller's response.

§ 08

Personal Data Breach

HelveX maintains an incident-response programme designed to detect, investigate, and contain Personal Data Breaches in a timely manner. The programme is described in detail on the Security page (Section 11) and in the Trust Center (Section 10).

Notification

HelveX will notify the Controller of a Personal Data Breach affecting Personal Data Processed on behalf of the Controller without undue delay and in any event within 72 hours of confirmation of the breach. Notification is delivered through the Controller's designated administrative contact (and through any additional channels specified in the Order Form).

Commitment

HelveX commits to notify the Controller of a confirmed Personal Data Breach affecting Personal Data Processed on behalf of the Controller within 72 hours, consistent with GDPR Article 33.

Content of notification

The notification will, to the extent of the information then available to HelveX and consistent with Article 33(3) GDPR, describe: (a) the nature of the Personal Data Breach, including, where possible, the categories and approximate number of Data Subjects concerned and the categories and approximate number of Personal Data records concerned; (b) the name and contact details of HelveX's point of contact for further information; (c) the likely consequences of the Personal Data Breach; and (d) the measures taken or proposed to be taken by HelveX to address the Personal Data Breach, including, where appropriate, measures to mitigate its possible adverse effects.

Ongoing updates

Where the information cannot be provided at the same time, HelveX will provide updates as new information becomes available. HelveX will preserve evidence relating to the Personal Data Breach and cooperate with the Controller's investigation, supervisory-authority engagement, and Data Subject communications as reasonably required.

No admission

HelveX's notification of a Personal Data Breach is not an admission of fault or liability and is made for the purposes of supporting the Controller's compliance with Articles 33 and 34 GDPR and equivalent provisions.

§ 09

DPIA and prior consultation

HelveX provides reasonable assistance to the Controller, taking into account the nature of the Processing and the information available to HelveX, in carrying out a data protection impact assessment under Article 35 GDPR and in conducting prior consultation with the Supervisory Authority under Article 36 GDPR, where the Controller determines that such an assessment or consultation is required.

Materials available

To support DPIA exercises, HelveX makes available: this DPA together with its annexes; the Privacy Policy; the Security page; the Trust Center; the model cards for each production model; and, on reasonable request and under MNDA, the current SOC 2 Type II report, ISO 27001 statement of applicability, and penetration-test summary. These materials are intended to provide the information the Controller needs to characterise the Processing for DPIA purposes.

Cost

DPIA assistance directly responsive to the Controller's good-faith DPIA exercise is provided at no additional charge. Where the Controller requests bespoke materials or extensive engagement beyond reasonable assistance, the parties will agree on reasonable charges in advance.

§ 10

International transfers

HelveX may transfer Personal Data outside the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland only in accordance with this section.

Mechanisms

Each transfer of Personal Data to a third country relies on one of the following mechanisms: (a) an Adequacy Decision applicable to the destination country; (b) the Standard Contractual Clauses, supplemented by any required addendum (UK Addendum / IDTA for UK-origin data, Swiss Addendum for Swiss-origin data); or (c) a derogation under Article 49 GDPR for occasional, non-systematic transfers where strictly applicable.

SCC Modules

The applicable Modules of the Standard Contractual Clauses for transfers under this DPA are: Module Two (Controller to Processor) where the Controller is established in the EEA and Personal Data is transferred to HelveX as Processor in a third country; and Module Three (Processor to Sub-processor) where HelveX engages a Sub-processor in a third country. The selection of Modules, the parties' identities, and the data-transfer-specific information are set out in Annex IV (SCCs Reference).

Supplementary measures

HelveX has assessed the laws of importing countries and implemented supplementary measures where the assessment identified residual risk. These measures include AES-256 encryption at rest with HSM-backed key management; TLS 1.3 encryption in transit; segregation of administrative access; pseudonymisation where feasible; and contractual commitments to challenge unlawful disclosure requests. The assessment is updated when material changes in importing-country law come to HelveX's attention.

Swiss Addendum

For Personal Data originating in Switzerland and subject to the Swiss FADP / nFADP, the Standard Contractual Clauses are supplemented by the Swiss Addendum recognised by the Swiss Federal Data Protection and Information Commissioner. References in the SCCs to GDPR are deemed to refer also to the relevant provisions of the Swiss FADP / nFADP; references to the supervisory authority and to courts are deemed to refer also to the Swiss FDPIC and Swiss courts as appropriate.

§ 11

Audits

HelveX makes available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with the obligations of Article 28 GDPR and allows for and contributes to audits, including inspections, conducted by the Controller or an auditor mandated by the Controller, subject to the conditions in this Section 11.

Standard audit pathway

HelveX's standard audit pathway is to make available, under MNDA, the most recent SOC 2 Type II report, ISO 27001 certificate and statement of applicability, and a summary of the most recent independent penetration test. These reports cover the controls relevant to the Controller's audit interests and are produced by independent third parties qualified to attest to those controls. The Controller agrees that, absent specific concerns not addressed by these reports, the standard audit pathway satisfies HelveX's audit obligations under Article 28(3)(h) GDPR.

Additional audits

Where the Controller has specific concerns not addressed by the standard audit pathway, the Controller may, no more than once per twelve-month period (or more often where required by Applicable Data Protection Law or in connection with a Personal Data Breach affecting the Controller), conduct an additional audit. Audits will be conducted (a) with reasonable advance notice (at least thirty (30) days); (b) during HelveX's business hours; (c) under appropriate confidentiality protections; (d) by an independent third-party auditor mutually acceptable to the parties; and (e) in a manner that does not unreasonably interfere with HelveX's business operations or those of other customers.

Cost

The Controller bears its own costs of any audit beyond the standard audit pathway. Where the audit results in a finding of HelveX's material non-compliance with this DPA, HelveX will reimburse the Controller's reasonable audit costs and remediate the non-compliance.

Scope limitations

Audits do not extend to: (a) data of other HelveX customers; (b) confidential information of HelveX not relevant to the Controller's audit interests; (c) HelveX's source code (HelveX may make summary information available where necessary to demonstrate compliance); or (d) information that would compromise the security of the platform.

§ 12

Deletion and return of Personal Data

On termination or expiration of the Terms (or the relevant portion of the Services giving rise to the Processing), HelveX will, at the Controller's election, delete or return all Personal Data to the Controller.

Process

For thirty (30) days following termination, the Controller may export Personal Data through the workspace or the API. After the export window, HelveX will delete Personal Data from active production systems within thirty (30) days. Personal Data persisting in backups will be deleted in accordance with the backup-retention cycle (default 30 days); during the residual backup window, the data remains encrypted and inaccessible to ordinary access paths.

Legal retention

HelveX retains copies of Personal Data only to the extent and for the period required by applicable law (in particular, retention of accounting records under Article 958f of the Swiss Code of Obligations). Where the Controller is not the entity to which such legal retention obligations apply directly, the retention is limited to information genuinely required for HelveX's own compliance, not as a general archive of Personal Data.

Confirmation

On Controller's request, HelveX will provide written confirmation of deletion of Personal Data, identifying the categories of Personal Data deleted, the date(s) of deletion, and any residual retention with the legal basis therefor.

§ 13

Special Categories of Personal Data

The Services are not specifically designed for the Processing of Special Categories of Personal Data and the parties do not contemplate, at the outset of the engagement, that the Customer will submit Special Categories to the Services. If the Controller intends to Process Special Categories using the Services, the Controller represents and warrants that such Processing complies with Article 9 GDPR (in particular, that the Controller has a lawful basis under Article 9(2) for Processing the Special Categories) and with any equivalent provision of Applicable Data Protection Law.

Where the Controller submits Special Categories, the Controller is responsible for ensuring that the configuration of the tenant is appropriate to the additional risk (including stronger access controls, more restrictive retention, and consideration of the zero-retention posture for inference traffic). HelveX makes available the technical capabilities to support these measures; the Controller is responsible for selecting and configuring them.

§ 14

Liability

The liability of the parties under this DPA is governed by the limitation of liability provisions in the Terms (Section 14 of the Terms of Service). Without limiting the foregoing, nothing in this DPA limits or excludes a Data Subject's rights under Article 82 GDPR or equivalent law to compensation from a controller or processor for damage suffered as a result of an infringement of Applicable Data Protection Law.

Where Article 82 GDPR or equivalent law renders a party liable to a Data Subject for damages, the parties' allocation of responsibility as between themselves is determined by the limitation of liability and indemnification provisions of the Terms, subject to the mandatory provisions of Applicable Data Protection Law.

§ 15

Term

This DPA enters into force on the Effective Date of the Terms (or, where executed separately, the Effective Date of this DPA) and remains in force for the duration of the Terms. Provisions of this DPA that by their nature should survive termination (including those concerning deletion and return of Personal Data, confidentiality, audit support relating to historic Processing, and liability) survive termination of the Terms.

§ 16

Governing law

This DPA is governed by the laws of Switzerland, harmonised with the GDPR for EU/EEA Personal Data and with the UK GDPR for UK Personal Data, and applied in conjunction with the Swiss FADP / nFADP for Personal Data originating in Switzerland. The substantive obligations imposed by Article 28 GDPR and equivalent provisions of Applicable Data Protection Law apply as mandatory provisions, regardless of the parties' choice of law, where such law would otherwise apply.

Disputes arising out of or in connection with this DPA are subject to the dispute-resolution provisions of the Terms (Section 19 of the Terms of Service).

§ 17

Order of precedence

In the event of conflict between this DPA, the Standard Contractual Clauses (where applicable), the Terms, and any Order Form, the order of precedence is: (1) the Standard Contractual Clauses (as to matters within their scope, where applicable); (2) this DPA; (3) the Terms; (4) the Order Form. The Order Form takes precedence over the Terms only as to those matters that the Order Form expressly addresses. Any provision in any document that purports to alter the substantive content of the Standard Contractual Clauses is void.
