Prometheus 2.0 — agents that run for hours.

Our biggest model update of the year. Plan-and-critique runs by default; multi-hour runs are deterministic and replayable; humans stay on the irreversible decisions through new approval-gate primitives.

  • ▸4–8× longer run horizon over Prometheus 1.x — single runs that go end-to-end on a workday.
  • ▸Native sub-agents for parallel branches — investigate three leads at once, merge the answer.
  • ▸Approval gates on tool calls, file writes, and sends — opt-in per project, default on for shared spaces.

Release cadence

Two clocks. Monthly. Continuous.

Updates land on two rhythms. The Workspace Refresh is the headline cycle — one named cycle a month, with the changes batched and written up. Underneath that, model and platform changes land as they're ready, with a short note on this page for each one.

Cycle 01 · Monthly

The Workspace Refresh.

A named cycle once a month. The biggest single landing point for new workspace surfaces, new agent capabilities, new connectors, and the meaningful re-tunes across the three models. Every Refresh ships with a written changelog and an engineering note explaining the why behind the bigger items.

Schedule: last week of the calendar month · numbered cycles · always announced here first

Cycle 02 · Continuous

Inline model updates.

Between Refreshes, the models change as they're ready. Ether, Nexus, and Prometheus each move on their own clock. When an underlying model is re-tuned, the change lands on this page with a short note on what improved, what regressed against the eval, and any setting that customers should reconsider.

No silent upgrades · the model your agent is pinned to does not move without a note here

How to follow updates

Pick the channel that fits your week.

Three ways to keep up. Pick whichever one you'll actually read — none of them are exclusive, and the content is the same across all three.

Channel 01

Monthly digest by email.

One email per Workspace Refresh, plus a short Monday round-up if anything material moved underneath. The subscribe form sits at the bottom of every page — same address as the footer.

Channel 02

RSS feed.

Every entry on this page lands in the feed within minutes of going live. Plug it into your reader of choice — the feed has the full text, not just the headline, so you do not have to leave the reader to scan a release.
