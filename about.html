The story

Three minds. One frontier.

We started HelveX because the first generation of AI products treated intelligence as a single setting on a single model. That works for demos. It does not work for the kind of work that has to land — the brief that goes to the board, the agent that runs while you sleep, the support reply that ships in under a second.

So we built three. Ether for the work that has to feel instant. Nexus for the work that has to be right and well-sourced. Prometheus for the work that has to actually go and do the thing. A router picks one when you don't want to choose, and you can override the router on any turn.

Around that, we built the workspace, the knowledge surface, and the trust layer that turn a model into something a team can rely on. Replayable agent runs, citation-grade retrieval, residency in your region, and a refusal calibration we measure as carefully as the capability.

We are based in Zurich, with research in EU-Central and engineering across three continents. We hire senior, we ship every week, and we publish methodology — including what didn't work — on the Research page.

How we operate

Four working principles.

We don't have a mission statement on the wall. We have four operating principles that show up in every code review, every research write-up, and every customer conversation.

01

Ship work, not slogans.

A real change in the product beats a real change in the marketing site. Every claim on this site is something we can show in the workspace.

02

Disagree with evidence.

We argue from data, written specs, and reproducible experiments. Strongly held opinions are welcome — they need to survive a numbered eval.

03

Trust is technical.

We treat security, residency, refusal calibration, and audit logs as engineering problems with measurable answers, not as marketing categories.

04

Senior, small, calm.

We hire people who have shipped before, give them the room to do it again, and protect deep work from meeting culture. Headcount is a last resort, not a goal.

How we engage

Scope, architect, build, operate.

Every engagement follows the same four-step shape. A senior engineer owns it end to end, and a written brief lands on your desk before any code does. Six to ten weeks for the first cycle, then a flat monthly retainer in operate-mode if you want us to keep the lights on.

01 · Scope

Written brief first.

We define the problem, the data sources, the constraints, and the success bar in plain English. Fixed price and fixed timeline come back inside one week — no metering, no surprises.

02 · Architect

Pick the model. Map the agent.

We choose Ether, Nexus, or Prometheus per task, draw the agent graph, and mark every irreversible step with an approval gate. Citations, replay budget, and refusal calibration are designed in, not bolted on.

03 · Build

Ship every week.

Code lands in your repo from week one and runs in your AWS or GCP tenant. We pair on the eval set, write the runbook beside the code, and the senior engineer who scoped the work is the same one shipping it.

04 · Operate

A monthly Workspace Refresh.

Flat monthly retainer. We tune prompts as models change, add tools as the work changes, and hand back a written cycle report. No lock-in — the IP, the code, and the eval sets are yours from day one.
